TODAY’S SCOOP:

While Texas’ energy officials have rightly pointed to fossil fuel shortages as responsible for the state’s blackouts, Gov. Greg Abbott has been appearing on Fox News blaming wind turbines and renewable energy for the state’s woes, even though as the New York Times notes, Texas’s “widespread electricity failure was largely caused by freezing natural gas pipelines” in a state where fossil fuels are “responsible for the majority of Texas’ power supply.”

Abbott’s goal is clear: he wants to use the disaster to pretend, in his words, that “the Green New Deal would be a deadly deal for the United States of America.”

Following the money offers one reason why Abbott is so determined to concoct such a ridiculous narrative and vilify the Green New Deal: he’s vacuumed in huge money from the fossil fuel industry he’s defending and that is at the heart of the disaster.

In all, Abbott raked in a whopping $28 million from oil and gas industry donors during his political career, according to data compiled by the National Institute on Money In State Politics. That includes more than $11 million in his most recent reelection campaign. Donations include a $1 million check Abbott received from Midland Energy founder Syed Javaid Anwar, and $1.4 million from billionaire oil magnate Ray Lee Hunt.

By contrast, Abbott has received just $70,000 from donors in the wind and solar industries.

Earther has more on the fossil fuel industry cash flowing to Republican officials who are trying to deflect blame from that industry in the wake of the disaster.

TODAY’S CLICKS:

• MINIMUM WAGE, MAXIMUM MISINFORMATION: A few years ago, the Congressional Budget Office undermined its own credibility by downplaying the catastrophic effects of climate change - now it is doubling down with a thinly sourced but widely cited report designed to sabotage the push for a minimum wage increase. Economist James Galbraith subjects the CBO report to some basic scrutiny and shows that the CBO “should not have published this report, and no one should rely on it.”

• U.S. ECONOMY WILL NOT ‘OVERHEAT’: Speaking of the CBO, economist Claudia Sahm analyzed separate CBO data and revealed that despite scaremongering about the U.S. economy, concerns about “overheating” are overblown. Sahm demonstrates that U.S. economic capacity has repeatedly been revised down over the years.

• HARVARD STUDY FINDS SLAVERY REPARATIONS COULD HAVE MITIGATED COVID DEATHS: A new peer-reviewed study from Harvard has found that had the descendants of enslaved Black people received financial reparations before the pandemic, Covid-19 transmission in Louisiana would have decreased by as much as 68 percent.

• FACEBOOK BLOCKS NEWS IN AUSTRALIA OVER GOVERNMENT PLAN TO FORCE PAYMENT TO PUBLISHERS: The social media giant announced Wednesday that it is blocking users in Australia from accessing news stories on its platform. It is also blocking users everywhere from accessing Australian news sources. The move is a response to proposed legislation to require social media companies to pay Australian news outlets posted on its site.
