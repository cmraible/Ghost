TODAY’S STAT:

The Partnership for America’s Health Care Future raised $55 million and spent $20 million attacking Medicare for All and a public option in 2019, according to documents obtained by The Daily Poster. Now, the health care industry front group is gearing up to fight a public option under the Biden administration.

TODAY’S WIN:

New York state is divesting its $226 billion pension fund — the country’s third largest — from fossil fuels, after nearly a decade of organizing and public pressure.

TODAY’S FLASHBACK:

In 1994, Biden’s HHS nominee Xavier Becerra endorsed a single-payer health care system on the floor of the House of Representatives, calling health care a “right, not a privilege.”

TODAY’S ACTION:

The Department of Justice has advised Trump against executing Brandon Bernard, who has been on death row in Indiana for half his life. He is scheduled to be executed tomorrow unless Trump is stopped from carrying out his ninth execution since July. Bernard’s defense team is asking that you email President Trump to demand he spare Bernard’s life.

TODAY’S CLICKS:

  • MORE EVIDENCE THAT LINCOLN PROJECT WAS A GRIFT: Last month, we obtained data from a political firm that showed what a failure the Lincoln Project’s ads were (and that some even swayed voters towards Trump). A top Democratic Party’s top super PAC did its own analysis, and found they didn’t sway voters all (despite going viral on Twitter).
  • BLACKSTONE’S ELECTION INVESTMENTS PAYING OFF: Once again, Democratic Rep. Richard Neal is blocking federal efforts to stop health care providers from passing on large surprise medical bills to patients. Neal’s re-election campaign was bankrolled by Blackstone, a private equity firm that owns a hospital staffing company responsible for many surprise bills, as well as other lobbying groups that have opposed reforms to end the practice.
  • TRUMP ENTRENCHING ENVIRONMENTAL DEREGULATION: Trump is using his final days in office to entrench four years of work to dismantle environmental regulations, open more land for drilling and enable polluters. Check out this tracker of the Trump administration’s efforts to roll back environmental protections.
  • ANTI-POVERTY TAX BREAK ENRICHES BILLIONAIRES: The Trump administration’s “opportunity” zone program was intended to increase investment in poor communities. But the biggest winners have been billionaires, including Jeff Bezos, Elon Musk, and Cleveland Cavaliers owner Dan Gilbert, who have used the program to shield their assets from taxes by putting them in designated distressed communities. Some of Biden’s advisers reportedly like the tax breaks, and Biden has proposed reform, not repeal, of the program.
