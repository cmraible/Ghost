Stuff I’m reading:

• We Should Still Defund The Police (The New Yorker) — “It is clearly a craven gesture when multibillion-dollar corporations claim that ‘Black lives matter,’ even as they refuse their Black workers hazard pay, paid time off, or a living wage. Nevertheless, these élite searches for absolution from the sin of ‘systemic racism’ have reaffirmed that racism is not only about burning crosses and the N-word: it is also present in the housing market, in institutions of higher education, in the job market, and most certainly in policing and the criminal-justice system.”

• QAnon’s Coronavirus-Fueled Boom Is A Warning Of What’s To Come (Huffington Post) — A must-read on the intensifying storm of disinformation that is consuming American politics.

• Kamala Harris’s Uber Test (American Prospect) — “Even before the COVID pandemic, Uber and Lyft had been losing billions of dollars because of their predatory business model that massively subsidizes every passenger ride in their monopoly bid to undercut the competition. Having a friend in the White House would be a huge asset to Uber. And Harris’s record regarding ride hailing has shown her Janus-like ability to talk out of both sides of her conflicted temperament, appearing as either an overly cautious flip-flopper or a pragmatic politician, depending on one’s point of view.”

• Oil And Gas Groups Block Briefing For Officials On Health Impacts of Drilling (Colorado Newsline) — Even in a blue state that’s dealing with huge wildfires, the war on science continues.

• Quest, LabCorp, And Slow COVID-19 Results(Acute Condition) — Monopoly power is one of the great problems of our time — this piece shows how it affects COVID testing. Fascinating.

• How Can We Plan for the Future in California? (The Atlantic) — California is on the frontlines of the climate crisis. Can it recover?

• Your ‘Surge Capacity’ Is Depleted — It’s Why You Feel Awful (Elemental) — As I wrote this week, I’ve found myself struggling at times. I periodically feel exhausted and overwhelmed in a way I never have before — and I know many others are feeling the same way. This article offers some answers about why that is.

• Fleeing the Trolls for the Grizzly Bears (New York Times) — This piece is right — if you need a break, the best break is to just try to get yourself into nature.
