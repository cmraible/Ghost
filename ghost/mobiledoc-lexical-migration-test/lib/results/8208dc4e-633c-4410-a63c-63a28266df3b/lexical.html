The TMI Weekend Reading List — 6/7/20

Stuff I’m reading:

• Will Urban Uprisings Help Trump? Actually, They Could Be His Undoing. (Mother Jones) — An important historical analysis of where we are now.

• Injustice, Double Standards and Heartbreaking Bigotry (Lexington Herald Leader) — Rex Chapman was one of my favorite NBA players when I was growing up. He also has one of my favorite Twitter feeds these days. Read his oped about this moment we are living through.

• David Frum’s Hold Over the Center (New Republic) — So-called “Never Trump” Republicans do not wield any power in today’s GOP — but they do wield influence in the Democratic Party, which is a terrifying thing when you consider their underlying political ideology.

• The Generation Shaped By Layoffs (GEN) — Millenials are often lampooned and belittled, but on the whole, they have it far worse than previous generations.

• Pentagon Wants to Predict Anti-Trump Protests Using Social Media Surveillance (Vice) — This 2018 article is newly relevant as the Trump administration tries to crack down on dissent and protest.

• George Floyd: ‘I’m gonna change the world’ (Houston Chronicle) — A must-read biography of the man whose death sparked a global uprising against racism.

• After Floyd Killing, We Need A Truth And Reconciliation Commission (USA Today) — An argument for using a process that South Africa used to try to change a racist culture and begin a healing process.

• Donald Trump Is an Autocrat. It’s Up to All of Us to Stop Him. (The Intercept) — The great James Risen sounds an alarm. This is not a drill. This is real.

• Shouting Into the Institutional Void (The Atlantic) — “The difference between 1968 and 2020 is the difference between a society that failed to solve its biggest problem and a society that no longer has the means to try.”

Stuff to Listen To:

• The Climate Deniers Have Lost Their King, and Still They Soldier On (Drilled) — A deep dive into the history of science denial, from one of the best podcasts out there.

• Technology and Surveillance Meet the Shock Doctrine (INET) — An interview with Naomi Klein and Avi Lewis about the COVID version of disaster capitalism.
