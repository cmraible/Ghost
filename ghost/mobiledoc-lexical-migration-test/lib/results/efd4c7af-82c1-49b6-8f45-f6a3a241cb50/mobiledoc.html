Good things are happening! Researchers have found that Indigenous resistance movements have stopped or delayed significant amounts of new greenhouse gas emissions over the past decade. A representative from Arizona gave an earful to the press about army generals going on TV to defend  the war in Afghanistan. And the labor movement is turning up the heat, again, on Senators who refuse to end the filibuster to pass the PRO Act.

All that and much more in this week’s Weekend Reader, exclusively for paying subscribers below.

Things That You Love To See:

• Get The Generals Off TV(Politico) — Ruben Gallego, a Democratic representative from Arizona and former Marine infantryman who served in Iraq, had some searing words for American generals appearing on cable news to criticize President Joe Biden’s withdrawal from Afghanistan. “They conducted the war incorrectly... They hid real-time data from Congress and the American public about how weak the Afghan government was, and now they still come out as if their hands aren’t involved in the problems that they created,” he said.

Tip Jar

• Federal Judge Strikes Down Trump Rule Governing Water Pollution (The New York Times) — “A federal judge on Monday struck down a Trump-era environmental rule that drastically limited federal restrictions against pollution of millions of streams, wetlands and marshes across the country. The Biden administration had already begun the lengthy process of undoing the policy, which President Donald J. Trump established in 2020 after farmers, real estate developers and fossil fuel producers complained that Obama-era rules had saddled them with onerous regulatory burdens. Mr. Trump’s policy allowed the discharge of pollutants such as fertilizers, pesticides and industrial chemicals into smaller streams and wetlands.”

• Congressional Democrats Call For Repeal Of Fossil Fuel Subsidies In Infrastructure Bill — More than 50 Congressional Democrats sent a letter to House Speaker Nancy Pelosi and Majority Leader Steny Hoyer calling for them to end fossil fuel subsidies as part of the bipartisan infrastructure bill. “We are writing to request that you include the repeal of fossil fuel subsidies in the Build Back Better Act,” the letter said. “In his fiscal year 2022 budget, President Biden committed to the inclusion of $121 billion in revenue raised from repealing fossil fuel subsidies, which includes $86 billion from tax breaks for foreign oil and gas income… Congress must follow through in implementing the President’s vision.”

Organizing Works:

• Indigenous Resistance Stops Emissions (Indigenous Environmental Network and Oil Change International) — “In this report, we demonstrate the tangible impact these Indigenous campaigns of resistance have had in the fight against fossil fuel expansion across what is currently called Canada and the United States of America. More specifically, we quantify the metric tons of carbon dioxide equivalent (CO2e) emissions that have either been stopped or delayed in the past decade due to the brave actions of Indigenous land defenders. Adding up the total, Indigenous resistance has stopped or delayed greenhouse gas pollution equivalent to at least one-quarter of annual U.S. and Canadian emissions.”

• AFL-CIO Chief Warns Of Election Consequences For Pro-filibuster Democrats(The Hill) — “AFL-CIO President Liz Shuler warned Tuesday that Democrats could face consequences at the ballot box if they don’t support nixing the legislative filibuster to pass a sweeping pro-union bill and other Democratic priorities,” The Hill reported. “Elected officials, if they’re not listening, that’s when elections end up having consequences,” Shuler told reporters.

• Portland Denies Zenith Energy’s Essential Certification(Oregon Public Broadcasting) — “The city of Portland dealt a big blow Friday to an oil-by-rail operation when it denied an essential certification for Zenith Energy’s controversial Northwest Portland oil terminal,” Oregon Public Broadcasting reported. The decision followed three years of organizing to stop the project.
