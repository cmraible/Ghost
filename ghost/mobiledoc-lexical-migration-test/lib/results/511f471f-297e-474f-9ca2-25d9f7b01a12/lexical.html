Things That You Love To See:

• Judge Rejects Billionaire Phil Anschutz’s Tax Lawsuit(Colorado Sun) — “A Denver District Court judge Thursday dismissed a lawsuit filed by billionaire Phil Anschutz and his wife, Nancy, seeking a nearly $8 million tax refund from theColorado Department of Revenue.”

• ExxonMobil Booted From Climate Nonprofit — “BOOTED,” tweeted journalist Antonia Juhasz. “Exxon was suspended from the Climate Leadership Council today: ‘After careful consideration, we have decided to suspend ExxonMobil’s membership in both the Council and Americans for Carbon Dividends, our advocacy arm,’ Chief Executive Officer Greg Bertelsen said today.” Of course, Exxon should never have been a part of the climate nonprofit in the first place, but better late than never!

• SEC Chair Gary Gensler Wants Climate Risk Disclosures By Year End(Radical Compliance) — “SEC chairman Gary Gensler said today that he wants a draft proposal for mandatory climate risk disclosures by the end of this year, and dropped more hints than ever before about what that proposal might entail. Gensler made his remarks on a webinar hosted by Principles for Responsible Investment, a think tank that supports more consideration of ESG issues in investment decisions.”

• Federal Trade Commission Knocks Facebook—After Facebook suspended the accounts of researchers at New York University, Acting Director of the Bureau of Consumer Protection Samuel Levine wrote a letter to Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg criticizing the company for invoking a Federal Trade Commission (FTC) consent decree as its justification for suspending the accounts. “The FTC is committed to protecting the privacy of people, and efforts to shield targeted advertising practices from scrutiny run counter to that mission,” Levine wrote. You love to seethe FTC doing its job!

Organizing Works:

• Cori Bush’s Protest Helped Win An Extended Eviction Moratorium (The Intercept) — As Democrats in Congress and the White House pointed fingers at one another for allowing the eviction moratorium to expire, Rep. Cori Bush of Missouri led a protest on the steps of the Capitol that forced President Joe Biden to act. She spoke with The Intercept about her efforts.

• Amazon Launching New Union Drive On Staten Island — “Amazon warehouse workers at JFK8 in Staten Island say they have signed more than 1,000 @amazonlabor union authorization cards & formed a giant worker committee,” tweeted VICE reporter Lauren Kaori Gurley, who has been covering labor organizing at Amazon.

• Democratic National Committee Staff To Join Union(New York Times) — “Staff members at the Democratic National Committee are set to be represented by a union, the first time a national party organization will have a unionized workforce, committee officials said on Tuesday… ’The D.N.C. has the ability to be a really powerful agent of positive change for working Americans, and we think this is an opportunity for us to really live those values,’ said Christen Sparago, who manages monthly donors for the committee and helped lead the unionization effort.”

• Biden To Extend Pause On Student Loan Payments — “Your ‘Happy Friday’ News: The Biden admin will extend the freeze on federal student loan payments to Jan. 31, per sources familiar with the plans,” tweetedNew York Times education reporter Erica Green.
