Things That You Love To See:

• A Senate Holdout Now Backs The PRO Act: Maine Independent Sen. Angus King had previously refused to support landmark legislation to make it easier for workers to join unions. But as a coalition of unions and DSA have intensified their grassroots campaign for the bill, King has now signed on as a co-sponsor.

• AOC Slams Dems’ ‘Gift To Billionaires’: U.S. Rep. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez was one of only two Democratic lawmakers from New York who courageously refused to sign a letter threatening to take down President Biden’s infrastructure and tax bill unless it includes a repeal of the federal cap on state and local tax deductions. "I think it's just a giveaway to the rich," she said, "and I think it's a gift to billionaires." Notably, the Democratic lawmakers are not merely pushing to raise the so-called SALT cap, but instead they want to fully repeal it — which means AOC’s statements are absolutely correct. The progressive-leaning Center on Budget and Policy Priorities has reported that “the top 1 percent of households would receive 56 percent of the benefit of repeal, and the top 5 percent of households would receive over 80 percent of the benefit.”

• IRS Budget Could Finally Get A Boost: The Daily Poster has been reporting on how IRS budget cuts over many years have reduced audits of the super-rich and corporations, helping create a huge gap between taxes owed and taxes paid. Now, the Biden administration is proposing a big increase in the IRS enforcement budget.

• Amazon To Face Shareholder Vote: Though Amazon managed to avoid a vote on a worker safety initiative, SEC regulators rejected the company’s attempt to block a vote on a separate measure to create more independent oversight of the company.

• Voters Want Wall Street To Stop Funding Fossil Fuels: A new poll shows a majority of Americans support policies to limit investments in fossil fuel projects that are exacerbating the climate crisis.

Organizing Works:

• NY Progressives Defeat Cuomo On Taxes: For years, New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo has protected his billionaire donors from modest tax increases. But now, New York’s progressive lawmakers finally forced him to enact significant tax reforms to make the rich start paying their fair share.

• Lawmakers Take Aim At ‘At-Will’ Employment: In These Times reports that “a group of worker centers, unions, community groups and policy organizations in Illinois officially formed a new coalition, Stable Jobs Now, that aims to dramatically shift the power balance between workers and bosses by eliminating ​’at-will’ employment — the practice that allows employers to fire their employees on a whim… The Stable Jobs Now coalition is pushing for passage of the Secure Jobs Act, a bill recently introduced in both chambers of the Illinois General Assembly. The legislation would make Illinois the second state to adopt a just cause system. Only Montana currently restricts at-will employment, a law dating back to 1987.”

• New Copper Mine Halted: Indian Country Today reports that “the Fond du Lac Band of Lake Superior Chippewa is breaking new ground in its fight against a planned $1 billion PolyMet copper mine in northern Minnesota. Asserting its rights as a “downstream state” under the Clean Water Act, the Fond du Lac Band filed a federal lawsuit in 2019 against the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers and the Environmental Protection Agency. The Army Corps of Engineers responded by issuing a decision temporarily halting wetlands permits the agency previously provided to the Polymet Mining Corporation that had allowed it to move ahead with the mine project.”

• A Huge Win On Voting Rights: The Appeal reports that “Tarra Simmons became the first formerly incarcerated lawmaker in Washington State’s recent history after she won a seat in the state House in November. Five months later, she is celebrating her first win as a legislator: A bill she sponsored was signed into law yesterday by Governor Jay Inslee, who credited her advocacy. It enables all formerly incarcerated Washingtonians to vote.”
