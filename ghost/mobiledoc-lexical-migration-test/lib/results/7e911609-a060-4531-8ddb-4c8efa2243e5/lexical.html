THINGS THAT YOU LOVE TO SEE:

  • Bernie Sanders Chairs Budget Committee(New York Times) –– With the Democrats back in control of the Senate, Sen. Sanders is now the chair of the Senate Budget Committee, and is already wielding that power to push for an emergency universal healthcare program during the pandemic.
  • Could Pension Funds Divest from Blackstone?(Daily Poster) –– As we predicted in last week’s TGIF, New York lawmakers and a national advocacy group are calling upon pension funds to review their investments in Blackstone, in light of Blackstone CEO Stephen Schwarzman’s role in bankrolling the insurrection.
  • Democrats Prioritizing Democracy Reform(Center for Public Integrity) –– One of the top items on the Democrats’ legislative agenda is the For The People Act, which is a key step towards revealing the dark money moving through our political system, The Daily Poster previously reported.
  • Will Blue States Tax The Rich? (Huffington Post) –– States with Democratic governors and legislatures are considering legislation to tax the rich in order to close budget deficits. It’s going to be an uphill battle, but organizers have been putting in the work.

ORGANIZING WORKS

  • Stimulus Proposal Contains Eviction Moratorium Through September(CNBC) –– Included in Biden’s latest financial relief package proposal is an extension of the federal eviction moratorium through September, following calls by activists to extend the moratorium.
  • Biden Appoints Financial Regulator To Hold Wall St Accountable(Bloomberg) –– Biden is reportedly going to appoint Gary Gensler, who has a reputation for taking on Wall St., as chair of the Securities and Exchange Commission. It’s a big victory for groups that have been pressuring the Biden administration not to appoint industry-friendly people to key regulatory posts.
  • These States Are Leading The Way On Environmental Justice(Inside Climate News) –– While there has been no action to address climate change on the federal level for years, organizers are pushing legislation at the state level that, if passed, would tackle climate change in frontline communities.
  • New Year, New Minimum Wage Increases(Economic Policy Institute) –– It’s nowhere near enough, but on January 1, the minimum wage increased in 20 different states. President-elect Joe Biden, meanwhile, is calling for a $15 minimum wage as part of his stimulus proposal. We have years of grassroots organizingto thank for that.

TAKE ACTION

  • A coalition of groups in New York want you to sign this petition to close a state budget deficit by taxing the rich, not cutting Medicaid.
  • The Sunrise Movement is having a day of action on January 21, Biden’s first full day in office, to kick off the “decade of the Green New Deal.” They want you to join them.
