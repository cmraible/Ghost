Good things are happening! Coal miners take to New York’s streets to speak out on BlackRock and their Alabama employer. Meanwhile, the fight to end fossil fuel projects scores a local victory, plus more people are getting COVID-19 vaccines. Finally, Marjorie Taylor Greene gets a taste of her own medicine — and she doesn’t like it.

Things That You Love To See:

• Marjorie Taylor Greene and Matt Gaetz’s Latest Stunt Completely Backfired (VICE) — “Before getting elected to Congress, Marjorie Taylor Greene spent her free time confronting a teen survivor of a school shooting on the street and calling him a ‘coward’ when he refused to engage with her. On Tuesday, she got a taste of her own medicine, and it turns out she doesn’t like it. The Georgia congresswoman and several of her colleagues had to end a press conference early on Tuesday after protesters drowned them out by blowing a whistle and yelling.”

• Vaccinations Rates Are On The Uptick(CDC Data) — Rates of people gettingtheir first dose of the vaccine in the United States are rising amid fears about the highly contagious delta variant and new vaccine mandates. Walmart, the biggest employer in America, just announced a vaccine mandate for its staff.

• The DOJ Won’t Let Rep. Mo Brooks Off The Hook For Allegedly Inciting The Jan. 6 Insurrection(Mic) — “Poor Mo Brooks. Hardly the sharpest tool in the GOP's legislative shed to begin with, the Alabama congressman who once tweeted his email password to hundreds of thousands of people was notified by the Justice Department on Tuesday that he's on his own when it comes to the ongoing lawsuit filed by his colleague, Rep. Eric Swalwell (Calif.). The suit alleges Brooks and other GOP figures were directly responsible for inciting the Jan. 6 attempted coup at the U.S. Capitol building.”

Organizing Works:

• County In Washington State Passes Nation’s First Ban On New Fossil Fuel Infrastructure(Earther) —“A refinery community in Washington passed a ban on constructing new fossil fuel infrastructure, becoming the first county in the U.S. to enact such a policy. It’s a policy exactly in line with what science has shown is necessary to meet the goals of the Paris Agreement.”

• Striking Alabama Coal Miners Taking Protest Back To New York(AL.com)— Striking Alabama coal miners traveled to New York City this week to picket the corporate office of BlackRock, the largest shareholder in Warrior Met Coal, the Alabama company the workers have been on strike against since April. You can donate to their strike pantry here and read excellent coverage of the strike here.

• How Unions and Their Allies Are Trying to Hold Amazon Accountable(Capital and Main) — “In California, labor advocates are supporting Assembly Bill 701, which would require all warehouse employers to provide workers with a written description of expected quotas, the number of tasks to be performed within a given time and potential adverse employment action. In other words, the employer has to share the metrics and the penalties for not meeting them.”
