TODAY’S STAT:

1.3 million Americans applied for unemployment last week — the 45th week in a row where more unemployment claims were filed than during the worst week of the Great Recession.

TODAY’S FLASHBACK:

Redditors shocked Wall Street (and the stock market) yesterday, successfully driving up GameStop’s stock price so much it nearly bankrupted a short seller hedge fund. Short sellers helped create the conditions for the 2008 stock market crash. Over a decade ago, then-Senator and longtime Biden adviser Ted Kaufman introduced a bill to crack down on predatory short-selling, but it didn’t go anywhere.

TODAY’S CLICKS:

  • LATEST ON SURVIVAL CHECKS: The White House is consideringpushing more “targeted” survival checks. Some economists and media outlets have started pressing Congress to restrict checks to individuals earning less than $50,000 and households earning less than $75,000 — which would cut out about half of all U.S. households. Meanwhile, progressives in the House are demanding monthly checks instead of one-time payments.
  • CORPORATE IMMUNITY KILLS: A new investigation from New York Attorney General Letitia James found that Gov. Andrew Cuomo’s administration underreported COVID deaths in nursing homes by nearly 50 percent. State assemblyman Ron Kim, who chairs the aging committee, said that Cuomo’s decision last spring to give nursing homes corporate immunity was “a business model soaked in blood.” Read The Daily Poster’s reporting series on Cuomo’s corporate immunity order here, here, here, here, here, and here.
  • “GREEN NEW DEAL” Biden’s climate policy is drawing heavily from the Green New Deal, but the president is not using that phrase. Whatever he wants to call it, Biden needs to frame climate policyin terms of jobs and racial and economic justice, advocates say.
  • INSURRECTION CASH: Corporations need to “carefully examine their millions of dollars in political spending from corporate funds, not just the more limited PAC spending,” according to an op-ed written by a Harvard Business School professor and government watchdog group. This is a key point, because the real money bankrolling the GOP goes to super PACs, not just direct campaign donations from corporate PACs that have suspended support for GOP politicians. Read The Daily Poster’s reporting on this here.
  • STILL COUNTING: The decennial census, which was supposed to be completed at the end of December 2020, is now expected to wrap up sometime this March due to pandemic-related delays. This has two important implications: First, it means that the Biden administration can take action to correct some of Trump’s most egregious efforts to undermine the census, and second, it means that redistricting has been delayed, throwing a wrench in campaigns preparing for the 2022 midterm elections.
