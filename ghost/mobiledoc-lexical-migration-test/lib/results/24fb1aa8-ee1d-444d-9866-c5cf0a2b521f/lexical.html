TODAY’S CLICKS:

  • SURVIVAL CHECK UPDATE: The $600 checks that were included in Congress’s coronavirus relief package are already reaching people, unless you filed your taxes with an online tax preparer. Now the question is when Congress will pass $2,000 checks, and how they will do it.
  • MANCHIN COULD BE A PROBLEM: Conservative Democrat Joe Manchin has reportedly been standing in the way of larger survival checks and fighting with Bernie Sanders about the idea, even though nearly 70 percent of his constituents make less than $75,000 a year. According to census data, nearly 13 percent of West Virginians are now suffering from food scarcity — and the number of people who say they often don’t have enough to eat has increased 70 percent during the pandemic.
  • A DAY ONE AGENDA FOR CONGRESS: Raphael Warnock and Jon Ossoff’s victories in Georgia will give the Congress the votes it needs to repeal some of the rules that Trump has passed as a lame duck.
  • DEMOCRACY REFORM: Democrats and Republicans now hold equal number of seats in the U.S. Senate, but the Democratic senators represent 41.5 million more people than the Republicans. That’s why key reforms like making D.C. a state and abolishing the electoral college (which makes it easier to steal elections) should be top priorities for this Congress.
  • UNIONIZE: Union busting firms are doing what they do best: surveilling employees and calling them “lazy” and “aloof,” Vice reports. As Trump proposes eleventh hour rules to fleece workers, it’s more important than ever for the labor movement to fight not just for legislative reform, but to bring democracy to the workplace.
