Good things are happening! House progressives say they’re ready to play hardball. Also, management at the New York Times accidentally showed its hand in a union-busting campaign, greedy landlords lose a legal battle, and one of the world’s biggest oil companies is finally being forced to pay for a spill. And did you hear? New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo will be gone in a week!

All this and much more in this week’s You Love To See It below, exclusively for paid subscribers!

Things That You Love To See:

• House Progressives Prepared To Wield Voting Power— Members of the House Progressive Caucus — Pramila Jayapal of Washington, Ilhan Omar of Minnesota, and Katie Porter of California — declared that they won't vote for the bipartisan infrastructure package unless the $3.5 trillion reconciliation bill also passes, a sign that they have learned a lesson about how to use their voting power to extract progressive demands. Let’s hope they stick to the plan…

• Federal Court Upholds Eviction Moratorium(The Hill) — “A federal judge in Washington, D.C., on Friday rejected a request from a group of landlords to block the Biden administration’s renewed eviction moratorium. The ruling by U.S. District Judge Dabney Friedrich, a Trump appointee, leaves intact the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention’s (CDC) extended freeze on evictions, which is set to run until early October.”

• Shell Has to Pay $111 Million for 1970 Oil Spill (Earther) — “The settlement in this case alone will not make up for the decades of environmental justice communities have suffered due to the fossil fuel industry. But it is at least a welcome start.”

• New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo Finally Resigns (The Daily Poster) — If you are reading this, you surely already know that Cuomo has put in his two weeks notice for his resignation as governor of New York. While resignation is not the same as facing accountability, and the political culture that enabled Cuomo remains in place, there’s no denying that this is good news. Organizing Works:

• New York Pension Fund To Consider Divesting From Gas, Shale Oil — In addition to announcing that the New York State Common Retirement Fund was divesting from five additional coal companies, state comptroller Thomas DiNapoli said in a statement that his office “is evaluating 42 publicly traded shale oil and gas companies to determine if they are prepared for the transition to a low-carbon economy…” DiNapoli announced late last year that the state would be divesting its pension fund, in response to years of activist pressure.

• NY Times Lawyers Accidentally Send Private Strategy Memo to Staff Union (The Daily Beast) — “The New York Times is engaged in a contentious back and forth with several hundred of the paper’s technology and product staffers who announced a unionization drive earlier this year. So it was likely a bit embarrassing when the law firm representing the paper’s management accidentally sent a private strategy memo to representatives for the newly unionized staff,” The Daily Beast reports.

• Rhode Island Activists Block Entry to Raytheon Facility to Protest Killing of Civilians (Truthout) — “A group of anti-war activists blockaded the entrances to a Raytheon facility in Portsmouth, Rhode Island, on Thursday morning to protest the role the weapons-maker plays in the killing of civilians in Yemen, the occupied Palestinian territories, and elsewhere around the world.”
