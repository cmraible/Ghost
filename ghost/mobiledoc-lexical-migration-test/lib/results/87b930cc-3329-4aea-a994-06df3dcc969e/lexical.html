This was one of those weeks that made very clear the work we are doing is having a real impact, but that as a whole, our country remains on a very dark timeline.

The good news — The Daily Poster got attacked by one of the major right-wing websites for daring to articulate the case for why Joe Biden and Democrats should support expanding the Supreme Court. So that’s some solid proof that the far right sees us having impact and sees us as a threat.

At the same time, I was on one of the fastest growing new podcasts to discuss our reporting on the Supreme Court. Meanwhile, none other than John Dean publicly touted our work and the New York Times followed up our reporting that broke open the big story of Amy Barrett’s familial link to an oil company that has a major climate case before the high court.

The bad news: the largest fire in the history of Colorado is currently bearing down on my community, and we just finished up a week that saw Republicans move forward with their plan to install another climate change denier on the Supreme Court, as Democrats basically surrendered. Oh, and most of the media spent far more time on emails about Hunter Biden than on a climate crisis that threatens all life on the planet.

I don’t know what to really say anymore, or where to look for sanity. When I log onto social media, I see a completely deranged society — there are right-wing MAGA trolls, and there are Brunch Liberals who spend their days glued to MSNBC and then posting their demands for retribution against progressives and former Bernie staffers, as the entire world burns.

Politics has become both disconnected from the world we all live in and genuinely psychopathic — and I find more and more days in which I wonder how much longer I will even be involved in any of this. The whole thing feels like a complete farce.

But then I spend time with my children and see my partner Emily canvassing for reelection in our community, and I remember that giving up is not an option — at least not yet. It’s why I’ve been spending 80 to 100 hours a week on trying to build The Daily Poster — because part of the solution is going to be grassroots-funded media that doesn’t have to answer to corporations and billionaires, and that can therefore try to spread the truth of what’s actually going on.

If we had a whole newsroom of reporters, we could make an even bigger impact than we are making now. I keep that vision in mind when I wake up every day and do this work, and I thank all of you for pitching in and being part of the team. Onward.

Rock the boat,

Sirota

After a bit of a lull in the spring, Encore and other debt buyers are back at it, filing suits by the thousands every week.”

• Black Americans Are Paying More To Own A Home And Falling Further Behind (CNN) — “Black Americans pay more than any other group to own a home, a disparity that contributes to roughly half of the $130,000 retirement savings gap between Blacks and Whites…Black homeowners pay more in mortgage interest, mortgage insurance and property taxes than other homeowners.”
