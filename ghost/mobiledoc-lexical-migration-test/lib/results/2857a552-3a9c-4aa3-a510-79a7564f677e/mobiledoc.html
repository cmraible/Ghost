Follow That Story:

• The Continued Push To Give Immunity To Nursing Home CEOs — We’ve been covering lawmakers’ push to shield nursing home execs from liability during the pandemic — and this Intercept story looks at the lobbying connection to Donald Trump.

• A Jackpot For Donziger’s Prosecutors: TMI busted open the story of a Chevron-linked private law firm being given the power to prosecute Chevron’s biggest critic — now new court filings show that law firm is making huge money off the case.

• Unemployment Systems Are Deliberately Slow: TMI recently reported on how unemployment systems have been deliberately rigged to delay and deny benefits — and now we see the real-world effects of that in Florida.

• Cuomo’s Refusal To Tax The Rich Now Hits Home: TMI has been reporting out the story of Andrew Cuomo protecting his billionaire donors from tax hikes — and now we see the real-worldconsequences of Cuomo’s intransigence.

Stuff I’m reading:

• Why I Will Be Voting 'No' On The Democratic Party Platform(Des Moines Register) — Pete D’Alessandro is a longtime Democratic campaign operative who I got to know during the Bernie 2020 campaign. He has written a powerful essay about why he is joining other progressives in voting against the Democratic Party platform.

• A Strained Symbiosis With Obama (Columbia Journalism Review) — “MSNBC was not and is not about to call Obama out for having broken his campaign promises of transparency, for permitting nine million people to lose their homes in the mortgage crisis, for failing to jail even a single banker, or for having failed to call the butchers of Iraq to account. There is no cable news station broadcasting today that is willing to state the blindingly obvious, to wit, that a less corrupt, less wealth-enslaved, less warmongering Democratic Party—a party that had paid more than lip service to the needs of working people over the previous eight years—would have walked away with the 2016 election.”

• It’s Now Even Easier to Fire U.S. Workers for What They Say (Bloomberg) — “Employers in the U.S. just got more latitude to fire employees who speak up about harassment and discrimination. They can already legally terminate people for almost any reason, including what they say at work. One of the few exceptions to that rule has been U.S. labor law protections for people protesting unfair workplace conditions. Now, companies have wider legal authority to axe those employees, too. New rules for employers come from a sweeping National Labor Relations Board ruling last week.”

• Public Colleges Hide Donors Who Seek To Influence Students (USA Today) — The most powerful forms of influence are those that operate invisibly, thereby bypassing the target’s cognitive filters that would alert them to the scheme. Billionaires understand this — and so they are making college campuses a target.

• The Simple Fix For Corporate Income Tax—Tax Stock Returns(Bloomberg) — Progressive economist Dean Baker argues that there is a far easier way to make sure corporations start paying their fair share of taxes.

• Life Is Now a Game of Risk. Here’s How Your Brain Is Processing It. (Elemental) — A thought-provoking look at how our brains are processing a reality that seems more fraught with risk than ever.
