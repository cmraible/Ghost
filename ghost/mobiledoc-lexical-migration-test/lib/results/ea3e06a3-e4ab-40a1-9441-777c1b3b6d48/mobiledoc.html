In 2019, conservative West Virginia Democrat Joe Manchin co-sponsored the “For the People Act,” his party’s landmark voting rights and democracy reform legislation. But sticking with his current plan to be the ultimate political wet blanket, earlier this week he announced he’ll oppose the bill this year because it “has garnered zero Republican support.”

Reform advocates are now working to change Manchin’s mind on the bill — and one of the main people leading the charge happens to have a long history with the West Virginia senator.

All of this and more in this week’s You Love To See It below, exclusive for paid subscribers!

Scoop That You Love To See:

Manchin Ally Pushes Him On Voting Rights Bill (The Daily Poster) — Mike Plante, who worked on one of Manchin’s gubernatorial campaigns, told The Daily Poster he is serving as a consultant to the West Virginia Freedom Alliance Action Fund, which has already run $220,000 worth of television ads pressing Manchin to support the “For the People Act,” Democrats’ landmark voting rights and democracy reform legislation. Read our full scoop on the matter here.

Things That You Love To See:

• Vast Majority of Voters Want Higher Taxes on Wealthy, Corporations — Nearly 7 in 10 voters support raising taxes on the wealthy and corporations, according to a new poll from ALG Research, Hart Research, and Americans for Tax Fairness. Moreover, voters become more supportive of President Joe Biden’s economic plans when they are told they will be funded by taxes on the wealthy and corporations.

• Senate Progressives Warn Joe Biden That He Needs Their Votes, Too (Huffington Post) —  Could progressive senators be learning a lesson from their moderate colleagues? “Progressive senators have so far sat quietly on the sidelines throughout infrastructure negotiations. But they’ve gotten more vocal this week about their priorities — such as investments aimed at electrifying the nation’s vehicles in order to combat climate change — being left out of any eventual deal,” Huffington Post reports. In the past, this has been an empty threat, but maybe this time we will end up pleasantly surprised.

• Biden Reverses One of Trump’s “Most Reckless and Irresponsible” Decisions (Earther) — “Conservation advocates are gearing up for a major win. On Friday, the Forest Service said it would “repeal or replace” a Trump-era rollback that allows companies to build roads and other infrastructure in much of America’s largest national forest. It could be [the] biggest victory for public lands the Biden administration has delivered yet — if the details pan out.”

Organizing (That We Hope) Works:

• Alabama’s Coal Miners Are Striking for Their Lives (The Nation) — Over 1,000 coal miners in Brookwood, Alabama have been on strike since April 1 of this year. “But I’m not going to give up, because that’s what they want,” one of the striking miners told The Nation.

• Thousands Came to Minnesota to Protest New Construction on the Line 3 Pipeline (Inside Climate News) — “By Monday night, some 200 protesters had been arrested as they attempted to stop the construction of Enbridge’s Line 3 replacement project. Many had chained themselves to pipeline construction equipment hoping to delay a project that they say would lock Minnesota — and the nation — into decades of continued burning of some of the world’s dirtiest oil and threatens the pristine waterways that many Indigenous people depend on for their livelihoods.”
