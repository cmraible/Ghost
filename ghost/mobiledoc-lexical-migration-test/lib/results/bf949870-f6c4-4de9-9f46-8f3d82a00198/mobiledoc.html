The TMI Weekend Reading List — 6/7/20

Stuff I’m reading:

• The Financialization of the American Elite (American Affairs) — This is one of the best articles I’ve read in a very long time. It traces the history of how our politics and culture have come to be dominated by Wall Street. Read the whole thing.

• The Looming Bank Collapse (The Atlantic) — Are we about to relive the 2008 financial crisis?

• Stop Losing: A Senator’s Battle Plan for Beating the Right (Crooked) — “Our worst trait as Democrats is our willingness to lose once we’re in a fight. What do I mean? We’re the party that achieved great field position on three key issues—immigration, climate change, and dark money—and all three times we walked away and conceded defeat.”

• Andrew Cuomo Saw COVID-19’s Threat to Nursing Homes. Then He Risked Adding to It. (ProPublica) — If you’ve been reading this newsletter, you know we broke some big news about New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo granting immunity to nursing home executives. This deep-dive from ProPublica provides a glimpse into how bad the situation in New York has gotten.

• The Democratic Establishment Is Getting Desperate (The Week) — As a follow up to TMI’s report on Friday about the situation here in Colorado, read this piece about how Democratic Party bosses are doing everything they can to destroy progressive primary challengers.

Stuff to Watch & Listen To:

• Are Wall Street Barons About To Destroy Your Retirement? (The Hill) — I was on Rising this week to discuss our big scoop this week about Trump helping private equity moguls raid workers’ retirement savings.

• Cautionary Tales (Tim Harford) — I recently started listening to this podcast series about stories from history that teach important lessons. It’s great.
