Stuff I’m reading:

• Congress Should Raise The Minimum Wage (Roll Call) –– Congress can raise the minimum wage, even without Republican support, through budget reconciliation. If they do, it would be the first time since 2009.

• Antonin Scalia’ Ghost Tries To Stop Student Debt Cancellation (The American Prospect)  –– A memo from the Department of Education asserting that cancelling student debt is not within the agency’s authority relies almost entirely on the legal decisions of the late Supreme Court Justice Antonin Scalia. “It’s fair to say that Antonin Scalia, from the grave, wrote this analysis,” The American Prospect reports.

• Biden Can’t Let His China Hawks Stop Climate Action (The New Republic) –– “A Biden administration can productively fight climate change or it can edge toward another Cold War, but probably not both.”

• Biden Considering Big Tech Lawyers For Key Antitrust Appointments(The Intercept) –– Biden is considering appointing Renata Hesse, a lawyer who has represented Google and Amazon, to lead the Department of Justice’s antitrust division. Hesse “worked alongside Sen. Ted Cruz defending Google a decade ago, helped shepherd through the Amazon/Whole Foods merger, and represented several pharmaceutical companies and other clients in antitrust cases.”

• Shots And Checks (The American Prospect) –– Congress should pass a bill quickly to send $2,000 checks to households and fund vaccine distribution, two policies that could help people immediately and pass with bipartisan support. These measures can’t wait for the long process of budget reconciliation that will be required to pass the rest of a much-needed economic stimulus bill.

• How Hitler Came To Power (The Nation) –– “In the late 1940s, many of the same leaders that Hitler had beaten down in the early 1930s returned to lead Germany, but now instead of arrogance and intransigence, these leaders of the left, right, and center, chastened by defeat and unprecedented destruction, showed a bit more humility and a willingness to compromise when discussing questions of constitutionality and policy.”

• San Francisco Giants Owner Backed Insurrection Caucus(Defector) –– Charles Johnson, “the 88-year-old billionaire and principal owner of the San Francisco Giants,” maxed out the contribution limit to a laundry list of Republicans who challenged the results of the election, have expressed support for QAnon, and espoused far-right views.

A message from Sirota:

Friends:

The transition from Trump to Biden has a lot of media outlets wondering about their future. Many have spent the last four years minting profits off either constantly promoting Trump or exclusively berating him, while ignoring everything else in American politics.

For The Daily Poster, the transition doesn’t require us to change much — we’ve been covering both parties from the beginning and that is what we will continue to do. But that is hardly a safe path. After the trauma of the Trump years, some left-leaning news consumers seem to crave their own version of Fox News that touts reassuring Democratic Party propaganda, and eschews reporting that might be inconvenient to Democratic politicians.

That’s not what we do — and frankly there are moments I worry that because we don’t do that, we could see less support for this kind of accountability journalism we are focused on. That said, this is a risk we have no choice but to take, because it’s not worth the slog of doing this work if you aren’t living up to some core principles, and our principles are clear.

In the last week alone, we’ve published scoops that Biden sycophants hate, and we’ve published reporting that Biden fans probably like. That’s because we are not committed to only publish bad or good news - we are committed to following the money, scrutinizing power and telling the truth, regardless of whether that truth is painful or enjoyable to one or another politician or political party.

That has always been our commitment to you — and we so appreciate you being part of the team. We could not do this work without our supporting subscribers — and we promise to keep holding power accountable, even as party control has changed in Washington.

Rock the boat,

Sirota
