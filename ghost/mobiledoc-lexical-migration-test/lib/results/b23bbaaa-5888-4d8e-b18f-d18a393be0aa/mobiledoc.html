The TMI Weekend Reading List — 5/24/20

Stuff I’m reading:

• Sure, The Velociraptors Are Still on The Loose, But That’s No Reason Not To Reopen Jurassic Park (McSweeney’s) — A darkly hilarious must-read on the perils of downplaying health concerns and prioritizing “reopening” the economy.

• Will Colorado Democrats Choose Another 1 Percenter For The Senate? (Colorado Sun) — Colorado is a blue state and Bernie Sanders won the presidential primary here. But Colorado is also a case study in how the super-rich can control Democratic Party politics.

• This Vermonter Is Attracting Support From Top Bernie Backers (The Nation) — Lt. Gov. David Zuckerman is one of the smartest, most committed progressive public officials in America. Read about his campaign to unseat a Republican governor.

• Another Real Estate Crash Is Coming (Jacobin) — The financial crisis was bad. Are we about to relive the same economic nightmare?

• After the Crisis, Big Business Could Get Even Bigger (American Prospect) — The COVID emergency may end up making big companies even bigger.

• A Dollar General Analyst Complained About Store Workers Getting Screwed. He Got Fired. (Mother Jones) — Another day in the life on the frontlines of the class war.

• We’re Not Polarized Enough (New Republic) — We tend to think of American politics as polarized, but it probably has to get more polarized before we find real consensus.

• The End of Meat Is Here (New York Times) — Lots of people roll their eyes at me and my fellow vegetarians, but our society really need to start thinking about changing our entire food system and our eating habits.

• What’s Next For Journalists? (Substack) — Some folks have asked me why I started this newsletter, and why I believe we need an entire new model for journalism. The good folks at Substack offer some answers about why  grassroots-funded media is so important to the cause of preserving and expanding a free press.

• From Camping To Dining Out: Here's How Experts Rate The Risks Of 14 Summer Activities (WBUR) — A handy guide about what kinds of activities are more safe and less safe in this pandemic.

Stuff to Watch & Listen To:

• Why Warren Is Shifting On Medicare For All (The Hill) — I talked to Krystal Ball and Saagar Enjeti about Elizabeth Warren’s rhetorical pivot away from Medicare for All — it appears she’s aiming to curry favor with Joe Biden in hopes of becoming his vice presidential nominee.

• The Jungle & The Pandemic (The Intercept) — A podcast episode that takes a look at the meat industry during the COVID crisis, and also offers a chat with Zach Carter about his new much-lauded new book on the life of John Maynard Keynes.

• Homewreckers (Reveal) — A podcast episode that reminds us about how the wealthy and powerful so often uses crises as opportunities to enrich themselves.
