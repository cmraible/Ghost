Good things are happening! In California, industrial spaces are being turned into parks and gardens, while natural-gas plants are being replaced by a massive build-out of zero-emission power projects. Oregon, meanwhile, just set some of the most ambitious carbon-reduction goals in the country. If that’s not enough, businesses spewing greenhouse gases into the atmosphere could soon be facing far more serious legal actions, courtesy of powerful new studies on climate change. Yeah science!

All this and much more in this week’s You Love To See It below, exclusively for paid subscribers!

Things That You Love To See:

• New Climate Science Could Cause Wave Of Litigation Against Businesses (The Guardian) — “Businesses could soon be facing a fresh wave of legal action holding them to account for their greenhouse gas emissions, owing to advances in climate science, experts have warned. More than 1,500 legal actions have already been brought against fossil fuel companies whose emissions over decades have played a major role in building up carbon in the atmosphere.”

• New York Ends Policy Of Suspending Driver's Licenses For Ticket Debt(Fines and Fees Justice Center) — “[On] Tuesday June 29, New York’s Driver’s License Suspension Reform Act takes full effect, ending the state’s widespread practice of suspending an individual’s driver’s license when they cannot afford to pay a traffic fine. This legislation will allow hundreds of thousands of New Yorkers to regain their driver’s licenses — and with it their access to work and other necessities.”

• California Just Approved A Massive New Build-out Of Renewables And Clean Storage(Canary Media) — “California regulators have approved a massive build-out of clean energy, batteries and an as-yet-undetermined mix of long-duration energy storage and dispatchable zero-carbon resources. The new capacity will replace natural-gas power plants and the state’s last nuclear power plant that are set to shut down over the next five years. And unlike previous proposals, Thursday’s order from the California Public Utilities Commission won’t require any natural-gas power plants in that mix.”

• Communities In Los Angeles Are Turning Industrial Sites Into Pockets Of Green(High Country News) — “This reuse of industrial space, often spearheaded by communities of color that have historically lacked easy access to parks and gardens, provides an inspiring blueprint for how to reclaim and replenish the land, both for ourselves and the generations to come, Los Angeles-based photographer Stella Kalinina told High Country News. ‘It’s a regenerative process,’ she said. ‘They are healing the land, healing our cities and healing people.’”

Organizing Works:

• The New Freedom Rides(The American Prospect) — “A cross-country bus caravan, led by Black Voters Matter and the UNITE HERE hospitality workers union, hailing from 21 states, converged on the U.S. Capitol on June 26 to fight back against a resurgence of voter suppression bills nationwide and advocate for federal voting rights legislation, as well as statehood for Washington, D.C.”

• These States Expanded The Earned Income Tax Credit In 2021 (Institute for Taxation and Economic Policy) — Washington and Missouri have passed legislation this year to implement a state-level earned income tax credit — a tax credit for low- to middle-income working people — while a handful of other states passed laws to increase the earned income tax credit or expand eligibility.

• Oregon Lawmakers Approve Ambitious Carbon-reduction Goals For State Energy Grid (Oregon Public Broadcasting) — “While at least 17 other states have similar goals, Oregon’s timeline of getting power suppliers to zero emissions by 2040 is more ambitious than nearly all. In addition to that end date, regulated entities would be required to submit plans to reduce emissions by 80% from a baseline amount by 2030 and 90% by 2035.”
