Things That You Love To See:

• Delaware Just Humiliated Its Democratic Senators(The Daily Poster) — “Less than two months after Delaware’s Democratic U.S. senators cast decisive votes to help Republicans kill a $15 minimum wage initiative, Democratic lawmakers in their own state just took a big step toward passing the same minimum wage legislation statewide. In the process, Delaware’ Democratic state lawmakers slammed the kind of corporate talking points that their own U.S. Senators used to justify their actions.”

• Biden Eyeing Income Tax Hike On Capital Gains(Bloomberg) — “President Joe Biden will propose almost doubling the capital gains tax rate for wealthy individuals to 39.6% to help pay for a raft of social spending that addresses long-standing inequality, according to people familiar with the proposal.”

• Company Behind Sinema’s ‘F*** Off’ Ring To Donate Proceeds To Minimum Wage Fight(The Independent) — “The jewellery company that makes the ‘Fuck Off’ ring worn by Senator Kyrsten Sinema has announced it will donate all proceeds from the sale of the accessory to an organisation fighting to raise the minimum wage. Over the weekend, the Arizona Democrat uploaded a photo of herself to her Instagram Stories, in which she could be seen sipping from a glass of sangria as showed off a gold ring featuring a rose and the words: ‘F*** Off.’”

Organizing Works:

• Jury Convicts Ex-Cop Derek Chauvin For Killing George Floyd (The New York Times) — While justice won’t come from an unjust system, the conviction of cop Derek Chauvin for killing George Floyd is undeniably good news. It is a rare moment of accountability for police murder, and indicates that the protests following George Floyd’s death may have helped turned the tides.

• Five Reasons to Celebrate Massachusetts’s New Climate Law(Union Of Concerned Scientists) — The Massachusetts legislature just passed a long-awaited climate change law that sets emissions-reduction goals, takes steps to protect environmental justice communities, boosts clean energy production, and incentivizes green transportation options. As an observer noted, “This new law, An Act Creating a Next-Generation Roadmap for Massachusetts Climate Policy, is the result of years of work, discussions, negotiations and a lot of effort from a myriad of organizations, legislators, coalitions and in general, people that care about a healthy and livable world now and in the future.”

• SEC Chief Gary Gensler Picks Top Labor Union Official for Policy Role(The Wall Street Journal) — “Gary Gensler, the new chief of the Securities and Exchange Commission, on Monday tapped a labor-union investment official as his policy director, raising expectations that the agency will embrace progressive policy goals. Mr. Gensler, who was sworn in on Saturday, picked Heather Slavkin Corzo for the top policy role in his office. The hire suggests Mr. Gensler will tackle issues such as stricter corporate disclosures related to climate-change risks and companies’ spending to influence politics.”
