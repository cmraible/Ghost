Stuff I’m Reading:

  • The Covid Vaccine Is Free, But Not Everyone Believes That (The New York Times) – Over the course of the pandemic, people have received bills for COVID tests that were supposed to be free, or had claims for COVID treatments denied that were supposed to be covered. As a result, as many as a third of unvaccinated adults fear that they will have to pay for the vaccine, even though they won’t. That could be deterring them from getting vaccinated.
  • The U.S. Government Hides Some Of Its Darkest Secrets At The Department Of Energy (The Drive) – “Over the last few years, allegations of secret, exotic technologies have reinvigorated claims that the [Department of Defense] may be concealing scientific breakthroughs from the American public. However, if the U.S. government, or some faction within it, hypothetically came across a groundbreaking development in energy production or applied physics, a very strong case could be made that such a revolution would likely be housed deep within the Department of Energy (DOE) rather than DOD.”
  • The Difficulty of Obtaining an Abortion in Mississippi Is By Design (The Flashpoint) – Are Mississippi’s abortion restrictions, at least in part, intentional policies designed to maintain a low-wage workforce in the state? For one sociologist, “it’s clear that the economic boon for Mississippi businesses provided by the state's restrictive reproductive healthcare laws is inextricably tied to the religious narrative behind them.”
  • For 5.1 Million Reasons, Cuomo's Book Was Appalling (Albany Times Union) – “But Cuomo wrote his book (supposedly) as the coronavirus pandemic was stealing lives and causing terrible pain. While a more responsible governor would have been totally focused on the pandemic and its devastation, New York’s instead leveraged the crisis for a massive payday.”
  • Why We Need Paid Leave After Miscarriage in the U.S. (Vogue) – Over one in ten pregnancies end in miscarriage, which can have serious mental and physical health consequences for parents who experience pregnancy loss. Yet, no laws currently exist in the United States to provide workplace protections or paid leave for people who experience miscarriage.
  • Democracy’s Moment of Reckoning (The New Republic) – “The For the People Act faces its own anti-democratic hurdle to passage—not one concocted by the Republican Party, but the legislative filibuster, a long-standing feature of the Senate that the Democratic caucus has the power to eliminate or change. Failing to do so would be more than a mere strategic blunder. The Republican Party is waging an assault on multiracial democracy, which even now, two decades into this new century, remains more aspiration than reality. The inequities of the American political system are already deep. If Democrats fail to act, the Republican Party will deepen them beyond hope of repair, securing minority rule for itself and bringing the project of American democracy to an end.”
  • Jamaal Bowman Is Right — the Left Should Draw a Line in the Sand(Jacobin) – “Bowman is exactly right: there is no substantive political case for bargaining down an already compromised infrastructure proposal. If the Biden administration is intent on pursuing such a course, it can and should suffer consequences for scaling down its own plans — and have to contend with lawmakers willing to vote 'nay' before allowing the bogus quest for bipartisanship to water down yet another major piece of legislation.”
  • The Lab-Leak Theory: Inside the Fight to Uncover COVID-19’s Origins (Vanity Fair) – “A months long Vanity Fair investigation, interviews with more than 40 people, and a review of hundreds of pages of U.S. government documents, including internal memos, meeting minutes, and email correspondence, found that conflicts of interest, stemming in part from large government grants supporting controversial virology research, hampered the U.S. investigation into COVID-19’s origin at every step. In one State Department meeting, officials seeking to demand transparency from the Chinese government say they were explicitly told by colleagues not to explore the Wuhan Institute of Virology’s gain-of-function research, because it would bring unwelcome attention to U.S. government funding of it.”
