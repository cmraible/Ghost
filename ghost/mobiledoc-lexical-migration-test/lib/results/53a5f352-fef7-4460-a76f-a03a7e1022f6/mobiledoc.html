TODAY’S CLICKS:

  • Pentagon Called Violent Insurrection “First Amendment Protests”: Right after the election, Trump purged the Pentagon leadership and installed political loyalists. Journalist David Cay Johnston points out that “Trump’s handpicked latecomers now running the Pentagon pooh-poohed the insurrection, calling it mere ‘First Amendment Protests’ in an official timeline of law enforcement and military inaction that day.” After Johnston’s report, Pentagon officials say the document in question “has been updated to more appropriately reflect the characterization of the events at the U.S. Capitol on January 6th.”
  • Far Right Planning Militant Action: Journalist Ryan Grim notes that plans posted online call for 15,000 people to block Democrats from entering the Capitol with the ultimate goal of stopping Joe Biden from becoming president later this month. A new FBI bulletin warns: “Armed protests are being planned at all 50 state capitols from 16 January through 20 January.”
  • Private Equity Is Laying Waste To The Economy: A new first-of-its-kind study finds that “private equity pillaging of the retail industry has cost over half a million jobs amid over 18,000 store closures through February 2020.”
  • Big Banks Temporarily Pause Buying Democracy After Riot: JPMorgan Chase, Goldman Sachs, Citigroup, and other major corporations that spend millions on U.S. elections and lobbying say they are pumping the breaks on political donations due to the Capitol violence. Many companies are only pledging to pause their direct corporate PAC donations — and for a short period of time — so it probably won’t mean much.
  • Trump Administration Plans to Designate Yemen’s Houthis as Terrorists: With Biden promising to end U.S. support for the Saudi-led war in Yemen, the designation could complicate the peace process. The Houthis are already facing U.S. sanctions.
