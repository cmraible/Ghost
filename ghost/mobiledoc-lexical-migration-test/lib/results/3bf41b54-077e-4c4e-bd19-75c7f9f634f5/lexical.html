The TMI Weekend Reading List — 7/12/20

Stuff I’m reading:

• A Plasma Shot Could Prevent Coronavirus. But Feds And Makers Won’t Act, Scientists Say (Los Angeles Times) — “Scientists have devised a way to use the antibody-rich blood plasma of COVID-19 survivors for an upper-arm injection that they say could inoculate people against the virus for months…Federal officials have twice rejected requests to discuss the proposal, and pharmaceutical companies have declined to design or manufacture the shots...industry executives have little incentive to produce the immunity shots for the coronavirus, given the possibility that a longer-lasting vaccine could replace it within a year.”

• Charles Booker Could Have Won (The Nation) — Progressive candidates can win tough races, but in many cases the resources and support needed to get over the finish line are arriving just a little too late.

• The Neoliberal Looting of America (New York Times) — A good primer on why you should be concerned about private equity taking over the world.

• Trump's Labor Board Eyes Striking Down FDR-Era Union Protection (TYT) — “President Trump’s labor board is considering killing a legal precedent that has protected unions since before he was born.”

• Carl Reiner’s Life Should Remind Us: If You Like Laughing, Thank FDR (The Intercept) — Carl Reiner’s life is a reminder that “an incredible amount of the development of American comedy, including the training and platforms that helped start the careers of every comedian above, can be traced directly back to the 1930s, when President Franklin D. Roosevelt’s New Deal provided government support for the arts.”

• The Lives Upended Around A $20 Cheeseburger (Washington Post) — I’m a vegetarian, but I am looking forward to reading this piece that traces the entire path of a cheeseburger.

• The Eye Of The Storm (The Critic) — A look at “the bruising experience of inadvertently becoming the target of a Twitter feeding frenzy.”

Stuff to watch/listen to:

• Old Sports Stuff On YouTube — It’s been tough to shut down at night. There’s so much going on, it’s just hard to turn off the brain. But I found one solution — watching old basketball games on YouTube. I’m watching my old 1991 Philadelphia 76ers. I’m sure you can find your favorite teams on there. If you’re having trouble, try it.

• The Vast of Night (Prime Video) — I love sci-fi films, and especially quiet, weird, mysterious sci-fi films. This was a pretty good one.
