Overview

The 1973 Monte Carlo ushered in the second generation and was no longer a hardtop, but a pillared coupe with rear side opera windows and frame-less door glass. New styling featured dual headlights with an egg-crate grille and Monte Carlo emblem in front, and vertical taillights above the bumper.

The 1973 Monte Carlo ushered in the second generation and was no longer a hardtop, but a pillared coupe with rear side opera windows and frameless door glass. New styling featured dual headlights with an egg-crate grille and Monte Carlo emblem in front, and vertical taillights above the bumper.

It came standard with radial-ply tires, Pliacell shock abosorbers, high-caster steering, and front and rear anti-roll bars.

A new model for 1973 was the Monte Carlo Landau that featured Landau vinyl roof, Turbine II wheels, and driver and passenger side sport mirrors.

It was named Motor Trend's "Car of the Year" due to its new styling and emphasis on Euro-style ride and handling.

The 1973 set a new production record for itself with nearly 250K being sold for the model year.
