The legendary 426 HEMI was no longer available for the 1972 Charger, and the top-of-the-line performance model R/T was eliminated. Because of economic crises, the Charger began to be marketed as more of a low-end luxury coupe with lower prices and waning power. Along with the R/T model, the Charger 500 and Super Bee models that had been offered were also eliminated. The only models remaining were the base coupe model, base hardtop, and the SE hardtop.

The SE model was a luxury edition that added pleated vinyl seats, vinyl roof, concealed headlights, and a Rallye gauge instrument cluster. It proved to be fairly popular.
