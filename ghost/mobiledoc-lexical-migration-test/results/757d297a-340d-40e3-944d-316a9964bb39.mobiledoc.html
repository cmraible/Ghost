After finishing up at Ramp House, I called up an Uber to make my way over to Miami Beach where the Showtime x Blackdove House was located. I walked up the stairs to the house excited to see what was being dreamed up inside. As I opened the door and entered the house the first thing I noticed was a giant aquarium in the foyer. Directly past that were a few folding tables with some people wired in and hacking away.

"Ahhh, this is amazing," I thought to myself. I felt like I was in Silicon Valley but I wasn't, I was still in Miami. The vibes were impeccable. People gathering together to build and iterate on ideas, it's exactly what the Miami Tech ecosystem needs to continue pushing forward. As Miami Hack Week organizer, Dave Fontenot has said to me and others, we need to enlighten engineers as to what's so special about Miami Tech. And what better way to do that than by bringing them here and giving them a taste of what we have to offer? I dig it!

Heading back inside I decided to sit down at one of the folding tables in the living room, which is where I met Simon. He started talking to me about what he and his team were hacking on, "We're building a TikTiok deepfake app," he said and of course I was instantly intrigued. "Who would you be deepfaking?"
