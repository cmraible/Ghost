You can derive an idea of your overall success in social media from your own site analytics. However, if you want to explore how your own social media activity is performing, there are a number of tools to explore which allow you to get insights into how your activity is contributing to your success - or not…

Twitter

Twitter has made its own analytics available to all users. Key measurements are:

  • Impressions - the number of times the tweet appeared in people’s timelines, and thus could have been read. It’s the maximum number of possible views - although the reality is probably lower as some people will have scrolled past without reading it.
  • Engagements - the number of actions taken on a tweet, such as clicking on a link,playing a video, retweeting it, replying to it and so on.
  • Engagement Rate - the percentage of impressions that led to an engagement.

Impressions are typically much lower than your follower counts, due to the time-based nature of Twitter.

Facebook

Facebook provides Insights into the activity on any Facebook Page (it does not do this for individual accounts). Key measurements are:

  • Page Likes - a measure of how the Page’s followers are growing - or declining.
  • Post Reach - the number of times a post (or group of posts) has appeared in someone’s timelines - typically much lower than your Like count.
  • Engagement - the number of times people have acted on a post, divided into Likes, Shares, Comments and Clicks.

Individual tabs give more insight into each of these measures and, in particular, to video views with a greta deal of detail on watch times, and when you lose people. Generally, Facebook as more information than average about the sorts of people accessing your content. Facebook provides its own guidance on using these metrics.

Instagram

Facebook has released Insights for Instagram Business accounts. You can convert your profile to a business account any linking it to your Facebook Page. They provide plenty of guidance on this.

Pinterest

Pinterest’s analytics get more useful when you add a registered site to them. They them allow you to see information on how much you content has been shared on Pinterest. In particular:

  • Impressions - number of times a pin has been viewed
  • Viewers - number of Pinterest users who have viewed the pins

They also offer information on Clicks, Repins and Likes for each page.

Bitly

Bitly is best known as a link-shortening service. However, the most useful element that many people overlook is that it also gives you detailed analytics on the links you share, including number of clicks and time of day when they were clicked. If you claim your website’s domain with them, it’ll also give you figures on other people sharing your content.
