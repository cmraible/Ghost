Segments

Classic - This segment is for collector cars that are at least 40 years old, and does not fall into any other hobby segment. For example, a 1963 Chevy Corvette would fall into the 'Sports' category also, but can also qualify as a classic by its age.

American Muscle - Encompasses muscle cars from all eras, including modern muscle. This is typically a two-door American car powered by a V8. Examples include Mustang, Camaro, Firebird, Charger, Challenger, Chevelle, and Nova.

Hot Rods - A modified classic, customized for performance. These cars are typically lightweight and two-door bodies, but the term is largely open for interpretation.

Sports & Exotics - A car that left the factory with performance, handling, and driver's experience in mind. Typically a two-seater, and can be from anywhere in the world. An exotic is an extension of the sports car niche, with characteristics that make the cars exclusive, limited, or unique.

Trucks & Off-Road - This category encompasses anything built on a pickup platform, SUVs, and 4x4 vehicles made for off-roading.

Imports & Tuning - Even if it's made in America, an import car is anything made under a company that is based overseas. A tuner car is an extension of the import category, which includes high-performance oriented vehicles.
