Things That You Love To See:

• Congressional Dems Plan To Question Exxon Lobbyist About Carbon Tax Video (Energy and Environment News) — “Congressional investigators are planning to question an Exxon Mobil Corp. lobbyist who inadvertently confirmed a decadeslong effort by the company to derail federal action on climate change in a secretly recorded video, according to officials involved in the effort. The interview by the House Oversight and Reform Committee will likely precede what stands to be a blockbuster hearing with Exxon CEO Darren Woods and other executives of major oil and gas producers.”

• Texas House Democrats Walked Out To Block Voting Restrictions Bill(Texas Tribune) — “Democrats in the Texas House of Representatives left the state Monday afternoon en route to Washington, D.C., in a bid to again deny Republicans the quorum needed to pass new voting restrictions with 26 days left in a special legislative session called largely for that purpose.”

• New FTC Chairperson Lina Khan Sends Staff Attorneys Job Hunting (National Law Journal) — “Now that the FTC has a Chair who is actually serious about cracking down on monopolies, some agency attorneys are revolving out to join giant corporate law firms,” the Revolving Door Project tweeted about the news.

• Clean Electricity Standard To Be Included In Budget Reconciliation Package (The Hill) — Sen. Tina Smith, D-Minn., said that a clean electricity standard will be part of the budget reconciliation package, which is likely to require utility companies to produce 80 percent clean electricity by 2030. Such a policy would save hundreds of thousands of lives in the United States over the next few decades by mitigating climate change and reducing air pollution.

• South Portland Wins Pipeline Lawsuit Over Local Clean Air Rule (Portland Press Herald) — “The city has prevailed in the lawsuit brought by the Portland Pipe Line Corp., ending more than six years of federal litigation over a local law that blocks the company from reversing the flow of its World War II-era oil pipeline to bring crude from Canada to Maine. The company gave up the fight Thursday, filing an agreement with the 1st U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals to voluntarily dismiss its appeal of a previous federal court judgment that upheld the city’s Clear Skies Ordinance.”

• The $1,400 Stimulus Checks Worked (Jain Family Institute) — “In short, stimulus checks worked. Nearly one quarter of families said they ‘mostly’ used their checks to increase their spending, and many other families spent some portion of their checks. Taken together, more than half of the $400 billion disbursed was spent within a few months of receipt. Families who did not use this assistance to increase their spending instead used it mostly to pay down debt (45%) or increase their savings (31%).”
