Overview

Not much changed from 1955 to 1956 with the Thunderbird, considering that sales for the Thunderbird were fantastic for its first year of production.

Not much changed from 1955 to 1956 with the Thunderbird, considering that sales for the Thunderbird were fantastic for its first year of production.

However, the few changes that Ford did make to the Thunderbird were significant. Removing the spare tire from the trunk to create more trunk space and moved it to a stylish, rear spare tire carrier.

The other addition to the Thunderbird Ford made was the iconic port window. Ford actually listened to what their customers were saying about how hard it was to see and created the port window, now one of the more iconic styling features of any 50's car.
