The TMI Weekend Reading List — 5/10/20

Stuff I’m reading:

• Screen New Deal (The Intercept) — My longtime friend and hero Naomi Klein on a new kind of shock doctrine unfolding during the pandemic.

• The real Lord of the Flies: What Happened When Six Boys Were Shipwrecked (The Guardian) — The novel “Lord of the Flies” presumes that humans will revert to their worst impulses during a crisis — but a real-life version of that novel proved that the opposite is also possible. You must read this story.

• Accepting Death Is Not An Option (Earther) — Brian Kahn writes: “The worst-case scenario with coronavirus is not mass death. It’s that people come to accept mass death—to accept that someone will die in the U.S. every 30 seconds as ‘just how it is.’ Yet that is the proposition being thrust on us now.”Democrats, It’s Time to Consider a Plan B

(New York Times) — The great Elizabeth Bruenig — who was a Pulitzer finalist for her reporting on sexual assault — argues that allegations against Joe Biden requires the Democratic Party to begin thinking about an alternative.

• Democrats Aren’t Stuck With Joe Biden (The New Republic) — Alex Pareene argues that the most respected and admired figures in the party could now coalesce around another path: Biden bowing out and the presidential contest continuing.”

• How My Boss Monitors Me While I Work From Home (New York Times) — A look at how employers can create a panopticon surveilling workers at all times during the work-from-home quarantine.

• The Risks - Know Them - Avoid Them (Erin Bromage) — A University of Massachusetts goes deep on what we really know about COVID.

• How ‘Never Trumpers’ Crashed The Democratic Party (FiveThirtyEight) — Somehow, a tiny group of right-wing Republicans who personally hate Trump have more influence over the Democratic Party than millions of progressives.

• The Tarkin Doctrine and the Sith Way of War (Blog Tarkin) — A look at the tension between religious zealotry, ideology and technocratic competence, through the lens of the Star Wars trilogy.

• Giant Hospital Corporation Takes Advantage of Coronavirus To Fight Nurses’ Union Drive (The Intercept) — The war on our frontline workers is intensifying.

Stuff to Watch & Listen To:

• The Bernie Blackout (Vice) — I cannot wait to watch this new documentary. I was interviewed for it, but haven’t seen the final product. It’s an important topic.

• Cuomo’s Approval Ratings Show Politics Is Getting More and More Shallow (The Hill) — I appeared on Rising with Krystal Ball and Sagaar Enjeti to discuss the New York governor’s popularity, even as he cuts Medicaid and presides over a disaster.

• Mitch McConnell's Calculated Decision To Become Trump's 'Enabler-in Chief’ (NPR) — The New Yorker’s Jane Mayer explains how Mitch McConnell has become one of the most powerful villains of the modern era.
