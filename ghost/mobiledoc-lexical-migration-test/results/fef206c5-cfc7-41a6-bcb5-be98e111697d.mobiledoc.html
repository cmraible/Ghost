Well, such is the case here. The Chevrolet Master (the HB) and Master Deluxe (the HA) are American passenger vehicles that were produced between 1933 and 1942 to replaced the Chevy Eagle. The Chevrolet Master was also one of the more expensive models in the automaker’s range at this time. A top-of-the-line Special Deluxe was launched from 1940 onward.

Regardless of the trim level, the HA cars sold over 302,000 units and were very popular with hot rodders. This is mainly because of the ease of availability when it came to parts, as well as the simplicity of replacing the engine.
