The 1985 Grand National remained largely the same with the exception of a revised front grille. The lack of changes stood as a testament to its engineering.

Almost all chrome was blacked out, and with George Throrogood's "Bad to the Bone" song ending up in the car's ad campaign, more and more youths were knocking on Buick's showroom doors leading the car to be more of an advertising platform than profit center.
