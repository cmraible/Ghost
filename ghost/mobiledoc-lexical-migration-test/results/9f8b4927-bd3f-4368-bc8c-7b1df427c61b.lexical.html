Stuff I’m reading:

• Biden Faces Pressure From Left Over Influence Industry Ties (NY Times) — Grassroots groups are pressing Joe Biden to block corporate lobbyists from running government agencies, if he is elected president. One problem: Biden has surrounded himself with lobbyists and corporate consultants.

• It’s The Healthcare System, Stupid (Le Monde Diplomatique) — The great Thomas Frank has a new book out about anti-populism. Read this essay about populism and health care.

• The Curse Of ‘Both-Sidesism’ (Grist) —“Ever wonder why Americans have been so slow to support climate action? A new study lays some of the blame on media bias —for 30 years, three of the country’s most influential sources of news gave too much credence to arguments that the world shouldn’t take decisive action to mitigate climate change.”

• McConnell’s Warning Of Lawsuit ‘Epidemic’ Undercut By Lack Of Litigation (Politico) — We’ve been aggressively covering corporate lobbyists’ attempt to use the pandemic to create liability shields for big business. One of their arguments has been that COVID will prompt an explosion of frivolous lawsuits. But the data show exactly the opposite.

• The $5.3 Trillion Question Behind America’s COVID-19 Failure (American Prospect) — A fascinating review of how the corporate obsession with stock buybacks undermines the response to the pandemic.

• Why Children of Men Haunts The Present Moment (New Statesman) — Alfonso Cuarón’s 2006 film has always haunted me, but it is particularly relevant to this moment we are living through.

• Seniors And Staff Caught In The Middle Of Nursing Homes’ Quest For Profit (The Guardian) — “The cycle of buying and selling care homes has led to shortcuts, closures, even fraud – and imperiled vulnerable residents’ health.”

• How Kamala Harris Fought to Keep Nonviolent Prisoners Locked Up (American Prospect) — “As California attorney general, she spent years subverting a 2011 Supreme Court ruling requiring the state to reduce its prison population. The overseeing judicial panel nearly found the state in contempt of court.”

• Why Did Labor Leaders Vote Against Medicare for All in the Middle of a Pandemic? (Jacobin) — A disturbing series of events went down at the DNC this week.
