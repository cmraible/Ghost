Overview

The Plymouth Duster was not changed significantly for 1972. New surface-mount side marker lights replaced the previous flush-mount items, the tail lamps became larger, one-piece units. The power rating of the 340 V8 was reduced from 275 hp to 245 hp due in part to a reduction in compression ratio from 10.2:1 to 8.5:1, as well as changing the intake valves from 2.02 in to 1.88 in. All horsepower rating numbers, even on unchanged engines, decreased for 1972 due to a new rating system. Chrysler's electronic ignition became standard on the 340 models in 1972.
