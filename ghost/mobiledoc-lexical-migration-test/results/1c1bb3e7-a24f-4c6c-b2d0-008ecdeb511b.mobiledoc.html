TODAY’S STAT:

Former Virginia Gov. Terry McAuliffe (D) lost a full third of the vote total President Joe Biden won in the state in 2020. According to the Virginia department of elections, McAuliffe won 1.6 million votes in 2021, while 2.4 million Virginians voted for Biden last year.

Fewer people typically vote in non-presidential year election cycles. However, Republican winner and private equity mogul Glenn Youngkin only saw a 300,000 vote drop-off from former President Donald Trump last year.

TODAY’S SCOOP:

The chairman of a firm that lobbies for Big Pharma raised almost $200,000 for House Democrats’ campaign arm in October, while party lawmakers were busy watering down their drug pricing plan and considering dropping it from their Build Back Better agenda bill altogether.

The new disclosure from the Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee (DCCC) shows that the drug industry’s influence-peddlers haven’t only been funnellingmoney to the individual lawmakers openly doing Big Pharma’s bidding in negotiations over Democrats’ reconciliation legislation — they are also bankrolling core party infrastructure. The principal mission of the DCCC, after all, is to support and elect House Democratic candidates.

Last week, the DCCC reported that Vincent Roberti, Jr. collected more than $190,000 worth of contribution checks for the committee in October. Roberti is the chairman of Roberti Global, which registered in May to lobby for Pharmaceutical Research and Manufacturers of America (PhRMA), the powerful Washington drug lobbying group. The firm recently disclosed lobbying for PhRMA on “issues related to H.R. 3,” the legislation that Democrats’ drug pricing plan was modeled on.

Roberti Global’s other clients include drugmakers Pfizer, Amgen, Ardelyx, and Astellas Pharma, as well as the drug distribution giant AmerisourceBergen Corporation. The firm also does work for the Biotechnology Innovation Organization, another lobbying group for pharmaceutical companies.

TODAY’S FLASHBACK:

On November 20, 1969, 89 Indigenous student leaders from universities across California took over Alcatraz Island under the unifying banner of “Indians of All Tribes.” Their 19-month occupation resulted in then-President Richard Nixon’s administration repealing detrimental termination and relocation policies that aimed for full assimilation, and political and cultural erasure, of Indigenous people. The Occupation of Alcatraz also helped catalyze the Red Power movement.

  • JOE BIDEN’S PRIVATE EQUITY VACATION: President Joe Biden and his family will celebrate Thanksgiving at the Nantucket, Massachusetts, vacation home of David Rubenstein, the billionaire co-founder of the Washington private equity giant Carlyle Group.   • PRIVATE EQUITY-OWNED NURSING HOMES SEE HIGHER COSTS, WORSE CARE: A new JAMA Health Forum investigation reveals higher Medicare costs and increased emergency room visits and hospitalizations for long-term residents in private equity-owned nursing homes, which make up five percent of nursing homes in the U.S. According to Mark Unruh, Ph.D., one of the Weill Cornell Medicine researchers, “Our findings indicate that private equity firm-owned facilities offer lower quality long-term care.”

  • SOUTHERN WHITE MIGRATION HELPS EXPLAIN TODAY’S POLARIZATION: New research identifies the outsized role the Great Migration of Southern whites in the 20th century may have played in shaping the United States’ current socio-cultural polarization.
