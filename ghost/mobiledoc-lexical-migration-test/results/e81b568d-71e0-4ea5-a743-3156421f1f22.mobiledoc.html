Overview

The 1974 Nova would mark the last year of the third-generation.

The 1974 Nova would mark the last year of the third-generation. It received larger parking lights and new bowtie grille emblems, as well as modified bumpers that added two inches to its overall length and helped cushion minor impacts.

The Powerglide was replaced by a lightweight version of the 3-speed Turbo Hydra-Matic 350.

Production reached almost 400,000; a result of an increasing trend from previous years.

The "Spirit of America" Nova was introduced in 1974 in anticipation of the US bicentennial in 1976. They were painted white and featured blue and red accent stripes as well as red and blue interior carpets and fabrics.
