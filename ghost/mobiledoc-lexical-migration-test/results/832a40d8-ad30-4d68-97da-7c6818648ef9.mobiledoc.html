Cities In Southern California Raise Wages For Frontline Health Care Workers

This week, Long Beach became the fourth city in Southern California to move forward with an ordinance to raise the minimum wage of health care workers in the private sector to $25 per hour. Rather than waiting to vote for the policy on a November ballot, the measure will be brought to a final vote before the Long Beach City Council next week.

After Los Angeles Mayor Eric Garcetti signed a $25-an-hour minimum wage ordinance for health care workers last month, the progress in Long Beach shows that the campaign led by the Service Employees International Union (SEIU) United Healthcare Workers West to establish a $25 minimum wage in ten Southern California cities is paying dividends.
