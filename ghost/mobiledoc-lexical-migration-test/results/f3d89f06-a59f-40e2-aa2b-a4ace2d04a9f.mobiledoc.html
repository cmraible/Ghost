Google's approach to product reviews

A couple of days ago, I linked to some news about Google updating how it will index reviews. Digiday has taken a deeper look at it, and to me, it looks like good news, but others clearly disagree:

Google said it will prioritize product reviews that feature information gathered through hands-on evaluation of the product, rather than aggregated information about it. The move stands to hurt publishers, especially those that have found success in aggregating product reviews.

So, Google is prioritising high quality, researched work over rapid aggregation of free low-quality content from the web? I know that this piece is trying to spin this as bad news, but I don't see it:

But it will also make commerce content more time- and resource-intensive to produce, said executives at several publishers with commerce content operations, so much so that it could make expanding into new product categories prohibitively expensive; well-researched, in-depth product reviews â€œare a hard operation to scale,â€ a source at a second publisher said.

Well, yes. Anyone who has been paying attention for the past 15 years will know that successful search-based techniques of low-quality content driving revenue tend to have a short shelf-life. Google will eventually penalise them. I'm struggling to find sympathy for publishers making such a basic mistake.
