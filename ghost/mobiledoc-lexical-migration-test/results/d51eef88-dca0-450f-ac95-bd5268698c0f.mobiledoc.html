The TMI Weekend Reading List — 6/27/20

Stuff I’m reading:

• Bernie Sanders Lost. But He Won. (The Atlantic) — “The greatest accomplishment of the Sanders campaign has less to do with moving good ideas out of the ‘radical’ category and into the mainstream and more to do with inspiring the people who will carry those ideas forward.”

• Last Night’s Elections Were a Political Earthquake (Jacobin) — A review of this week’s big electoral wins against the establishment.

• Andrew Romanoff’s Campaign for Climate Justice (The Nation) — Last week, we covered the U.S. Senate primary here in Colorado. This new piece by John Nichols takes a look at the June 30th primary’s implications for the fight against climate change.

• ‘No Cop Money’ Pledge Should Extend To Consultants & Super PACs (The Appeal) — A deep-dive into the ties between the Democratic Party’s consultant class and the police groups that influence elections.

• Wall Street Is Making Millions Off Police Brutality (Vice) — Police brutality bonds mean financial firms profit off police violence.

• Where Was the County’s Department of Public Health? (Capital & Main) — How local government fell down on the job, and created one of the bleakest chapters in Los Angeles history.

• U.S. Ranked Worst for Workers’ Rights Among Major Economies (Bloomberg) — A political system owned by corporations and billionaires has created a legal system that crushes workers.

• Stimulus Debit Cards Come With a Forced Arbitration Clause (American Prospect) — Predatory provisions that restrict Americans’ legal rights are now being promoted by the U.S. Treasury Department.

• How Amazon Used the Pandemic to Amass More Monopoly Power (The Nation) — Amazon’s quest to own and control everything is laying waste to small businesses and workers.

Stuff to Watch & Listen To:

• Wall Street Just Got A New License To Gamble During Depression (The Hill)— I was on Rising this week to discuss regulators’ move this week to weaken the so-called Volcker Rule, which was designed to prevent another financial crisis.

• Trump’s Grotesque Tulsa Speech and the Cult of Trump (Bill Moyers) — Bill talks to journalist Jeff Sharlet about Trump’s increasingly unhinged behavior.
