The Dodge Viper always complemented its brutal supercar power with the stunning looks of a concept car, and this beautiful 2006 Dodge Viper SRT-10 Coupe is a perfect example of both. The 2006 model year marked the introduction of the second-gen Viper coupe – now called the SRT-10 Coupe instead of the GTS – but it still had an instantly recognizable shape with the double-bubble roof mixed with the available Viper GTS Blue paint and wide, white racing stripes.

If you like what you see, GT Motor Cars has this amazing, low-mileage 2006 Dodge Viper SRT-10 Coupe listed for sale.

Many Vipers have been extensively modified and/or abused over the years, so it isn't very common to see one in as good of condition as this SRT-10 Coupe. This 2006 Dodge Viper has had just two owners in its lifetime, and it is showing less than 7,200 miles on the odometer. While it might look like a stock Viper from the outside, this SRT-10 is hiding a big secret under the hood.

Strapped to the front of the Viper's 8.3-liter V10 engine is almost $10,000 worth of Paxton Supercharger power. According to Paxton, this unit increases the standard output of the Viper (510 horsepower and 535 lb-ft of torque) up to 700 horsepower and 633 lb-ft of torque on 7-8 psi of boost. That's more than enough to give this venomous Viper even more bite!
