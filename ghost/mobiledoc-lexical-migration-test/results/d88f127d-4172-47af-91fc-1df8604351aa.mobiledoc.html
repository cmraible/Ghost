Overview

The 1969 DeVille was restyled in the Eldorado image with Eldorado-like front fender treatment that helped emphasize a stronger horizontal design line. Rear quarters were extended to give the car a longer look. An all new grille was released with dual horizontal headlamps positioned in the outboard step down areas of the grille. The hood was also extended, and the roofline was more square with the rear deck and bumper being more sculpted.

A new ventilation system was introduced because of the elimination of front vent windows that also provided a sleeker look and improved visibility. A model identification signature script still sat above the horizontal body side rub molding towards the back of the rear fenders.

The only engine available remained to be the 472ci/375hp V8 all mated to the Turbo Hydra-Matic transmission.
