If you were even mildly geeky and young in the 1980s, then you would have been deeply familiar with Crash and ZZap!64. They were part of the first wave of computer gaming mags, in a field largely dominated by more serious hobbyist mags. They were brash, swashbuckling, rude and huge fun.

I was a loyal reader of Crash, and it deeply shaped the way I think about publishing. At the heart of that was the artist Oli Frey, who created most of the covers, and a good chunk of the interior art. He passed away recently, and this is a lovely obituary of a man who was a genuine trailblazer in magazines.
