1958 Chevrolet Impala

The Impala was introduced in 1958 as a new, upmarket, sporty trim package. Unique to this model were its six taillights, which set it apart from lower trim levels with only two lights on a side, and this classic styling cue would become its trademark.

1963 Chevrolet Impala

This custom-built performance car is powered by a massive 655Ci V8 motor that features dual carburetors. Power is sent to the rear wheels through a reverse pattern T400 transmission. The T400 transmission is a favorite among drag racers across the country for its performance and durability. This car has a triple pass aluminum radiator, Dart aluminum block, MSD ignition system, Moroso crank trigger, Callies Magnum Plus crankshaft, Crane solid roller cam, fully CNC ported engine and many other upgrades. Paired with the T400 transmission is an FTI anti-balloon torque converter and an Earl 19-row transmission cooler.

1967 Chevrolet Impala SS 427
