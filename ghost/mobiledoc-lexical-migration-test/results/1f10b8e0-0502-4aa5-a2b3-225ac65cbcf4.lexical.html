The uneasy relationship between the media and Wikipedia

An interesting and characteristically angry piece from Mic Wright for his (inevitable…) Substack. The core of it is that our newspapers sometimes get very sniffy about Wikipedia and its reliability — especially when Wikipedia brands papers as non-reliable sources.

But, well, people in glass houses…

There's an interesting partner article to be written on this about how Wikipedia's reliance on a very narrow range of mainstream media sources is biasing the site, but not politically. Because the traditional media is somewhat myopic in what it covers — the sports it chooses to write about and those it doesn't, its long-term neglect of computer-gaming, even as it became a huge commercial and cultural force — Wikipedia ends up mirroring its choices. They need to start taking B2B and consumer magazines far more seriously as reliable sources.
