General Motors completely re-imagined the Corvette for its eighth-generation, moving the iconic American sports car more closer to supercar status. Giving the car its ability to sprint to 60-mph from a standstill in less than three-seconds, the 495-horsepower 6.2L V8 engine was moved to a mid-mount position. However, the move was not only about increasing the Corvette’s acceleration but also about optimizing weight distribution. This resulted in a new stance that benefited the new generation’s cornering and handling abilities. It also allowed the use of a more aerodynamic body style which also helps maximize the car's performance as well.
