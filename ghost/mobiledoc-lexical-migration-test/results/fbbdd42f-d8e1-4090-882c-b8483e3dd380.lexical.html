Are point and click games your specialty? They have a certain charm to them, don’t they? Sometimes all you need after a long day at is to sit down and engross yourself in a story that you are the pilot of. We’ve scoured the Nintendo Switch eShop high and low and we’ve found 35 of the best point and click games to help make sure that you choose the right one to unwind with.

This list has been updated as of June 2020.

2064: Read Only Memories INTEGRAL

Developed By MidBoss
Published By MidBoss
Rating M

Featuring nostalgically crisp pixel graphics, 2064: Read Only Memories tells the story of a sentient robot and a struggling journalist on a mission to uncover the secrets of Neo-San Francisco. With voice-acting from a handful of prestigious voice actors, HD Rumble and a Nintendo Switch exclusive side story, 2064: Read Only Memories is a great way to kick off this list.

The Adventures of Bertram Fiddle

Developed By Rumpus Animation
Published By Chorus Worldwide Games
Rating T

The Adventures of Bertram Fiddle is a hilariously comical adventure that takes place in a colourful Victorian London. With a clear Sherlock Holmes influence, Bertram Fiddle must follow the clues in order to uncover the mystery of Geoff the Murderer. This game features over 30 locations, wacky voice acting and an onslaught of head-shaking puns that will leave you smiling from ear-to-ear.

Afterparty

Developed By Night School Studio
Published By Night School Studio
Rating M

Who would’ve thought that Hell isn’t all that it’s cracked up to be? Lola and Milo are unexpectedly struck down in the prime of their lives and when they find themselves in the depths of the Netherworld, they must find their way back to the land of the living. Afterparty is one of those games that you’ll be looking back on with fondness for years to come.

Agatha Knife

Developed By Mango Protocol
Published By Mango Protocol
Rating T

Agatha Knife is a 2D point and click adventure about a young seven year old girl who works at her mum’s struggling butchers. She loves animals just as much as she loves killing them for their meat, but she doesn’t like the looks of terror the animals give right before the deed. So in order to rectify this and save her family’s business, she sets out to create her own religion – Carnivorism.

Apocalipsis: Wormwood Edition

Developed By Punch Punk Games
Published By Klabater
Rating T

What can make or break point and click games is the uniqueness in their presentation, so how about playing one entirely inspired by medieval artwork? Throughout this wondrous depiction of the of the 15th and 16th centuries, guide Harry through the strange lands in order to be reunited with lost love. Apocalipsis is a dark and thrilling tale that is only heightened by the genre.

Bad Dream: Coma

Developed By Desert Fox
Published By Forever Entertainment
Rating M

Are you ready for your bones to chill and your hairs to stand on end? Bad Dream: Coma may boast a minimalistic art style, but it doesn’t fail to deliver a tale that is brimming with hidden content and multiple story paths. If gripping horror is your game, then Bad Dream: Coma is the name of your next point and click adventure.

Developed By Telltale Games
Published By Telltale Games
Rating M

While we all lament the downfall of Telltale Games, the legacy that this company leaves behind is one that will forever influence this story-driven genre of games. Batman: The Telltale Series has you making the difficult decisions that Bruce Banner himself has to make and become the caped crusader now more so than ever.

Published By Bulbware

Burly Men at Sea

Developed By Brain & Brain / Seaven Studio
Published By Plug In Digital
Rating E10+

Burly Men at Sea follows three burly and bearded men during the early 20th century faring the Scandinavian seas. Encounter creatures from the area’s rich folklore throughout the game’s beautiful and simplistic art style. With branching story paths and each playthrough only last 30-45 minutes, Burly Men at Sea is a great way to wind down in between various other point and click games.

The Darkside Detective

Developed By Spooky Doorway
Published By Isometric Dreams
Rating E10+

Detective McQueen really has his job cut out for him this time! As the lead investigator of the Darkside Division, it is McQueen’s duty to protect the citizens of Twin Lakes from the spookies and the scaries that threaten the city’s harmony. With tongue-in-cheek humour that is sure to have you holding your sides, The Darkside Detective crams a lot of personality into its 3-5 hours of gameplay.

Dead Synchronicity

Developed By Fictiorama Studios
Published By Badland Games
Rating M

With the world in disarray after the “Great Wave”, humans are turning into what are known to be “the Dissolved”, causing those infected to do as their name suggests. You play as Michael, a man who has no recollection of his past on a quest to find out just what the hell as happened in a world he once called home. Dead Synchronicity is both gory and disturbing, so this one may not be for the weak of heart – you have been warned!

Deponia

Developed By Daedalic GmbH
Published By Daedalic GmbH
Rating T

The world is now a literal trash heap and those less fortunate are doomed to live among it while the upper class get to live in a utopia above the clouds. That is until one day, when a young lady named Goal falls from the sky, that Rufus seizes the opportunity to make for the land above known as Elysium. Deponia is both a hilarious and wondrous adventure that will pull on every emotion possible.

Goetia

Developed By Sushee
Published By Forever Entertainment
Rating T

Abigail Blackwood is a ghost, forced to live out her eternal consciousness in Blackwood Manor that occupies the ruins of Oakmarsh. Uncover the secrets of her missing family and of the manor’s hidden past. With a wide variety of puzzles, over 20 hours of gameplay and 115 rooms to explore, Goetia is a chilling adventure that stands among the best horror point and click games.

Grim Fandango Remastered

Developed By Double Fine
Published By Double Fine
Rating T

Speaking of the best point and click games, you cannot go much further than Grim Fandango! Manny Calavera is a travel agent at the Department of Death and he is tasked with selling luxury packages to souls that are about travel on a four-year journey to eternal rest. Having been awarded many game of the year awards from both its original release in 1998 and its remaster today, Grim Fandango is one that needs to be played by any fan of point and click games.

Guard Duty

Developed By Sick Chicken Studios
Published By Ratalaika Games
Rating T

Guard Duty is a tale as old as time itself; the beautiful princess has been kidnapped and it’s up to Tondbert, the lowly guard, to rise above adversity, find the evil that plagues the land, undo the wrong decision that altered the the course of humanity and sets to destroys civilisation as we know it almost one thousand years in the future and, wait… What were we doing again? Oh right, the princess! Inspired by classics of the genre, Guard Duty is a point and click adventure that takes you on a journey, spanning a millennium whilst not forgetting to have a laugh along the way.

The Journey Down Trilogy

Developed By SkyGoblin
Published By Blitworks
Rating T

The entirety of The Journey Down Trilogy has you traveling from the metropolis City of St. Armando to the the ancient jungles and temples of the mythical Underland. Kentucky Route Zero: TV Edition

Developed By Cardboard Computer
Published By Annapurna Interactive
Rating T

The critically acclaimed Kentucky Route Zero: TV Edition was initially released episodically over five instalments and now, the entire collection is in one download. Lost Horizon

The Lion’s Song

Developed By Mi’pu’mi Games
Published By Mi’pu’mi Games
Rating T

Set in early twentieth century Austria, The Lion’s Song tells the stories of struggling artists and brilliant minds struggling to overcome personal and social demons. The narrative spans across four episodes that each tell a personal tale: a famous composer struggling with writer’s block; a painter who struggles through his own challenges; a female mathematician living in a man’s world; and finalising each tale with closure. With a beautifully crafted art style and heart gripping stories to tell, The Lion’s Song is sure to paint perspective for all struggling artists alike.

Machinarium

Developed By Amanita Design
Published By Amanita Design
Rating E10+

Machinarium tells the story of a small robot who must brave the harsh environments of the scrap yard and return to the city in order to save his beloved.
