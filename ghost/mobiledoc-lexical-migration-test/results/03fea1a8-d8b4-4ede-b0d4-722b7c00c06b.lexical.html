Make Tom Selleck proud and place your bid on this two-owner retro Ferrari with just a 19,175 miles on the clock.

Back in the day Magum P.I., a popular television series that ran from 1980 to 1988, starred Tom Selleck as Thomas Sullivan Magnum IV, a private investigator in Hawaii who drove a bright red Ferrari. Frazier Motorcar Company is excited to offer the opportunity to take home this two-owner low-mileage 1982 Ferrari 308 GTSi.

The exterior of this 308 GTSi, designed by Pininfarina, is equipped with a targa top and is sprayed Rosso Corsa red, the customary national racing color of Italy. According to the description, the exterior paint is "extremely nice" and the body panel gaps are Ferrari factory perfect. Even the wheels and tires are correct for this 308 model, plus it has just 19,175 miles on the clock.

This retro Ferrari is powered by a mid-mounted naturally-aspirated 2.9-liter V8 engine backed by a 5-speed manual transmission that spins the rear wheels. The power output is 237-brake-horsepower at 7,000-rpm and 192 lb/ft of torque at 5,000-rpm. A fresh complete timing belt service was performed, and the car drives very tight and responsive without any extra noise, besides the rumble of those Italian eight cylinders.

Open the doors to an immaculate tan interior with upholstery and carpeting in fantastic condition. Even more, the dashboard is in exceptional shape and shows that the car was garage-kept and meticulously cared for.

The sale of the car comes with the receipt of the belt service as well as original manuals, books, original tool roll, and jack. The car also has a clean Carfax which shows the correct mileage as well as the two-owner history.
