This report was written by Julia Rock.

THINGS THAT YOU LOVE TO SEE:

• These Lawmakers Would Have to Resign From Corporate Boards If H.R. 1 Passes (Sludge) — The For The People Act, or H.R. 1, the democracy reform bill that passed the House of Representatives last week, would prevent lawmakers from sitting on the boards of for-profit corporations. According to an analysis by Sludge, this would require at least 15 members of the House to resign from board positions. (Senators are already banned from sitting on corporate boards while in office.)

• Sen. Bernie Sanders To Bring Amazon Union Fight To Washington (The Washington Post) — “Sen. Bernie Sanders (I-Vt.) will bring the high-stakes labor battle against Amazon to Washington, D.C., on Wednesday, when a union-supporting worker will testify before the Senate Budget Committee. The hearing, which will focus on income inequality, will include testimony from Jennifer Bates, who trains employees at Amazon’s warehouse in Bessemer, Ala. That’s the facility where 5,805 employees are in the middle of an election to decide whether they will be represented by the Retail, Wholesale and Department Store Union. Sanders has also invited Amazon founder and chief executive Jeff Bezos, the world’s wealthiest person, to testify at the hearing.”

• Permanent Infrastructure Investments In The American Rescue Plan (The American Prospect) — While the American Rescue Plan is full of many temporary provisions that must be made permanent to enact lasting change, The American Prospect reports that some of the infrastructure investments included in the plan could have long-lasting impacts. That includes investments in K-12 education, childcare, community health facilities, and state and local government aid which could be put towards local infrastructure investments.

ORGANIZING WORKS:

• Top Congressional Democrats Call for Gov. Cuomo's Resignation(CNN) — “The majority of House Democrats from New York, including prominent and powerful members of Congress, on Friday called on Democratic Gov. Andrew Cuomo to resign from office in the wake of sexual harassment allegations and his handling of Covid-19 deaths at state nursing homes. Thirteen House Democrats from New York on Friday said Cuomo must resign, arguing that the allegations have impeded his ability to effectively govern and serve the people of New York.”

• Biden Picks Anti-Monopoly Advocates For Top Regulatory Posts (The New York Times) — Biden is set to appoint Lina Khan, a leading antitrust lawyer who helped lead the House investigation into Big Tech power, to the Federal Trade Commission. He also appointed Tim Wu, Khan’s colleague, to the National Economic Council. “It gives hope that the last 40 years of consumer welfarism in antitrust could be on its last legs, and that we can start enforcing existing laws [with] vigor,” Zephyr Teachout tweeted about the appointments.

• Biden Administration Affirms Grad Students’ Right To Organize(National Labor Relations Board) — The National Labor Relations Board has withdrawn a Trump-era rule that would have excluded graduate students from being categorized as employees, preventing them from forming unions.

READER COMMENT OF THE WEEK:

Educate the people on the waste of the F-35, or the billions being spent on NEW nuclear weapons –– as if we need more than one to obliterate life. Organize, educate, agitate.”
