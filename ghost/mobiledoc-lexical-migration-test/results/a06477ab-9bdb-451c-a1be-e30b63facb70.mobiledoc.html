TODAY’S STAT:

Fewer than a third of voters want Pelosi to be reelected as speaker, while over 56 percent think she should be replaced, according to a new poll from Politico and Morning Consult. Unsurprisingly, Pelosi is much more popular among Democratic voters than Republican voters — but only about half of Democrats think she should remain speaker.

TODAY’S ACTION:

Fight for the Future wants you to sign this petition to pardon Edward Snowden, the NSA whistleblower who leaked documents to journalists which revealed the United States government’s massive surveillance apparatus. You can read more about the case for pardoning Snowden from Glenn Greenwald.

TODAY’S WIN:

The year-end omnibus includes a provision to phase out the production and consumption of a dangerous greenhouse gas, hydrofluorocarbons, or HFCs. According to Earther, “If passed, it would basically be the first new climate legislation in more than a decade to clear Congress.”

TODAY’S FLASHBACK:

Twenty-five years ago, the Clinton administration terminated the rule designed to force pharmaceutical companies to charge a “reasonable” price for medicines developed at government expense.

TODAY’S CLICKS:

  • DOJ SAYS U.S. GOVERNMENT CAN KILL ITS OWN CITIZENS: Last month, a Department of Justice lawyer argued that the United States government has the power to kill its own citizens without oversight in certain cases, during a hearing on a lawsuit filed by an American journalist who alleges he was placed on the U.S. government’s “kill list.”
  • BIDEN APPOINTS AUSTERITY ADVOCATE: Weeks after progressives celebrated blocking austerity ideologue Bruce Reed from being named Biden’s budget director, Biden selected Reed for a top White House job anyway. Reed, who headed an Obama-era commission to build support for Social Security cuts, will be Biden’s deputy chief of staff. Reed has also funneled millions of dollars into Betsy DeVos’s organizations through his own education reform group.
  • GOOD/BAD ON DIVESTING PENSIONS: The bad news is that Trump’s Department of Labor just finalized a rule to make it harder for private sector employees to divest their pensions from certain industries, which we wrote about earlier this fall. The good news is that Democrats in Congress have introduced two bills that would make it possible for 5.5 million federal employees to divest their pensions from fossil fuels.
  • US v. TECH MONOPOLIES: Tech giants are going to extreme lengths to fight antitrust action. Facebook and Google made a pact to work together in the face of an investigation into their monopoly behavior, and Facebook even offered to set up a competitor to its own business as part of an attempted settlement agreement.
