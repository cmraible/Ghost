Overview

Gone was the 427, in was Chevrolet’s new pavement pounding 454ci V8!

Gone was the 427, in was Chevrolet’s new pavement pounding 454ci V8! This was the biggest news for the 1970 Corvette. The four-speed Corvette manual transmission was made standard, replacing the former three-speed. Sales fell to nearly half, however, due to the strike at GM. A new “cheese-grater” grille insert and front fender vent distinguished the new Corvette model from the previous year. Only one Corvette with the LS7 (454/465hp) option was built. GM had banned ultra-high performance cars, however the LS7 option was listed in early 1970 sales brochures with none ever being sold.
