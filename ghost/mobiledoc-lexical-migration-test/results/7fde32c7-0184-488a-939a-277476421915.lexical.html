The Speed Society team created a poll to ask fans what car they should give away to one lucky winner. The answer was the Redeye. After scoping out dealerships across the country, they got their hands on a brand new 2019 Redeye Hellcat Wide-body finished in Destroyer Gray, a name that couldn't be more fitting for this modern Mopar.

Speed Society had Forza Tuning's Brenton Brown fly out to their headquarters in San Diego to start working his magic to turn this already-powerful car into the ultimate performance machine. "The goal was to create the most powerful and reliable Hellcat on the road," according to Speed Society. Off the assembly line, the '19 Hellcat Redeye Challenger already comes equipped with supercharged 6.2-liter V8 engine and generates 797-horsepower and 707 lb/ft of torque to the pavement.

On a mission to fix the shortcomings of the modern powerhouse such as the stock platform, engine, supercharger, suspension and rear end, Brenton went to work to tap into the car's utmost potential. Brenton added Forza's 1100-hp 4R package to ensure that this Hellcat-powered Redeye Challenger will feature asphalt-stomping power.

The Forza Tuning package consists of a plethora of mechanical upgrades including a Jokerz Performance ported and polished supercharger, a Griptec pulley, HP Tuners unlocked ECU, AT balancer, ATI crank pin kit, a Gates Supercharger "Green" belt, Milodan thermostat, Kenne Bell Dual Boost-A-Pump, Brisk spark plugs, 1300cc Injectors from Injectors Connection, American Racing Headers 2-inch primary long tubes with high-flow cats, AAD Challenger Wheel Hop Eliminator kit, Sophn rear sway bar link kit, Driveshaft Shop aluminum driveshaft, Per4mance Development DIRS Extreme rear diff brace, Forza dual-valve crankcase breather system, and a Forza supercharger spacer kit.

Once everything was finished in the performance department, the car was given a brand new set of Pilot Sport 4S rubber (295/35/ZR20 front, 305/35/ZR20 rear) from their corporate partners at Michelin. Even more, the gas tank was filled with VP Fuel before being strapped down on the Speed Society Mustang AWD dyno to make sure all 1,100 horses were galloping as they were meant to do.
