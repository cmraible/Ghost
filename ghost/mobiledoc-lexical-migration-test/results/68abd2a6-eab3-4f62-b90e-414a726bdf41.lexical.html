Chile’s Empowering Constitution

In Chile, a democratically elected convention proposed a new constitution that centers on environmental justice, gender equality, and worker empowerment. The document attempts to recognize Indigeneous autonomy and guarantees access to health care, abortion, housing, education, and pensions. This Twitter thread by a Green New Deal organizer with Rep. Cori Bush’s (D-Mo.) office translates and highlights what the constitution could mean for confronting climate change. The proposal emerges as a result of mass protests that started in 2019 against austerity and the Pinochet era.

Recent Economistarticles have attempted to discredit the new constitution. But as media critic Adam Johnson noted in response to these stories, “In defending the 1980 Pinochet-era constitution, The Economist, however, probably ought to note how much the magazine played an active role in not only overthrowing Chilean democracy 50 years ago, but how much it propped up and defended the subsequent dictatorship known for right-wing death squads and mass killings.”

The proposed new constitution is an exciting step that exemplifies what mass action can do to demand fundamental rights. It will be voted on in a mandatory referendum on September 4th.

California Will Produce Insulin To Tackle Prices

“Nothing epitomizes market failures more than the cost of insulin. Many Americans experience out-of-pocket costs anywhere from $300 to $500 per month for this lifesaving drug. California is now taking matters into our own hands,” Gov. Gavin Newsom (D) said in a recent video.

Insulin prices are astronomical. For reference, prices in the U.S. are almost four times as much as in Chile, which has the second highest prices of countries compared in a 2020 study. California has the highest number of new diabetes cases in the country.

In order to confront the market and price gouging, California will invest $100 million to develop much cheaper insulin and an in-state manufacturing facility. California’s investment is a helpful reminder that when it comes to basic health services people need, we should not be hostage to a rigged market setup.

Union Momentum Finds Strength In Numbers

Growing labor momentum is showing up in the data! This week, the National Labor Relations Board announcedthat, “During the first nine months of FY22, union representation petitions filed at the NLRB have increased 56 percent — up to 1,935 from 1,240 during the same period in FY21. At the same time, unfair labor practice charges have increased 14.5 percent — from 11,451 to 13,106.”

In addition to the NLRB report, a new study from Zoomshift shows Google searches for “join union” in five states have jumped: Oregon (up 126 percent), Kansas (124 percent), and Iowa (115 percent) top the list, along with New Jersey (96 percent) and Alabama (57 percent).

California To Send Out Inflation Relief Checks

Amidst the avalanche of inflation news, California has announced it will provide inflation relief checks of up to $1,050 per family with dependents. The checks are part of a larger $17 billion-dollar relief package that will suspend the state’s diesel sales tax and assist many in paying past-due utility bills. The relief will be issued as soon as October. About 23 million Californians are slated to receive checks, and contrary to what the Federal Reserve thinks, getting money into the hands of families and workers will be vital during these hard times.

Congressional Staffers Demand Climate Action

“We’ve crafted the legislation necessary to avert climate catastrophe. It’s time for you to pass it,” 200 congressional staffers wrote in a letter to Democratic leaders on Tuesday. The letter started circulating on Monday and quickly amassed support in lawmakers’ home districts.

“We are tired of the merry-go-round, and afraid of what inaction will mean for future generations and millions of lives all over the world,” one congressional staffer toldThe Independent. Earlier in May, staffers won a raise, demonstrating what their organizing can accomplish. Representing a much younger generation, they are demanding urgent climate action and their organizing efforts will be important to getting key climate policy over the finish line.
