TODAY’S CLICKS:

  • PROFITABLE COMPANIES LAY OFF WORKERS, ENRICH SHAREHOLDERS: Poverty rates in the U.S. have grown faster this year than any other year in recorded history, while 45 of the nation’s 50 largest public companies have turned a profit. How are they spending the cash? At least 21 of those profitable companies have laid off thousands of workers, according to a new Washington Post investigation.
  • THIS WEEK IN CLIMATE FINANCE: This week has been a mixed bag for action on climate from the government’s top financial regulators. The Fed joined the world’s largest central banks in committing to the goals of the Paris climate agreement, while the SEC finalized a rule to help shield oil, gas, and mining company payments to public officials from the public eye, which we first reported on last week.
  • HOUSE SPEAKER ELECTIONS: Speaker Nancy Pelosi has made valiant efforts to block progressives from influencing the proceedings of the House of Representatives. Rep. Alexandria-Ocasio Cortez says Pelosi needs to be replaced as speaker — but doesn’t know who should replace her, and is instead recommending progressives extract concessions in exchange for a vote for Pelosi.
  • STIMULUS TALK WHIRLWIND: Negotiations over an end-of-year stimulus package have been all over the place this week, will a deal expected soon. We’ve been tracking provisions that lawmakers are fighting over, like blanket legal immunity for corporations (which doesn’t seem to be in the final package) and a bailout for defense contractors.
