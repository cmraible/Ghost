You may have wondered what will be the best study apps for students in 2020? What we’re about to share in this article may serve as a very good indication:

College and high school students are constantly overwhelmed with a variety of tasks. They have to attend lectures, write countless research papers and essays, and prepare for exams. Besides, college life is completely different from what most students are used to. Your parents are not around anymore so there’s no one who could help you wake up in the morning, remind you of the necessary tasks for today, or check your homework. College students enter adult life so there’s no surprise that they are always stressed out.

 

Students have to master the art of time management and they need to plan their schedules to get everything done on time. However, this is not the only challenge. They also need to manage their budget, take notes, and plan their projects in advance, not to mention extracurricular activities that also take a lot of time and effort. Fortunately, there are many helpful apps that can help you stay organized and increase your productivity. Some of these apps will allow you to quickly schedule all of your daily activities, while others will take care of your budget or help you organize your study materials. Here are the five best study apps that will make your life in college or high school easier.

 

 

  • XMind
    One of the most important steps of many assignments is brainstorming. You need to find a good topic to write an interesting paper, and you should also generate ideas so that you can select the ones worth including in your paper. Brainstorming is a process of generating thoughts without evaluating them.
    XMind will help you manage your ideas and do research. This app will make your ideas flow naturally, organizing them on the go. It allows you to create various charts and to use templates. It has a free version and a pro version that costs $79.

 

 

 

  • Sleep Cycle alarm clock
    Maintaining a healthy work-life balance is crucial for your productivity. However, it’s difficult to get enough sleep when you have a lot of work to do. The first challenge is waking up. It may seem that it will be easier for you to wake up if you just sleep more, but in fact, the most important thing is your sleep cycle.
    Sleep Cycle is an app that analyzes data from your accelerometer and microphone to determine the time when you’re active. Based on this data, the app suggests when it’s better for you to sleep, taking into account your pre-defined parameters. The premium version also integrates with Philips Hue, tracks your heart rate, and features snore detection.

 

 

 

  • Dragon Anywhere
    Note-taking is one of all the students’ basic needs. You need to take notes in class or when researching your topic so that you won’t forget any important details when writing an assignment. However, you need to do it quickly, and it’s a challenge.

 


Dragon Anywhere will enable you to dictate your notes. Just speak into your device, and the app will convert your words into text. The app remains active as long as you speak and turns off automatically if you don’t speak for 20 seconds. Besides, it adds the most frequent words to your user dictionary. This app also supports voice commands. For example, if you want to delete the last piece of text, just say “scratch it.” You can also quickly send your text to other applications.

 

 

  • Tasker
    This mobile app will help you automate hundreds of tasks based on many different factors. For example, you’ll be able to turn off your smartphone’s ringer depending on your location so that nobody will distract you with a call when you’re in class. This app can also record your phone calls, check your voicemails and SMS, and send automated messages.

 

 

 

  • Evernote
    Evernote is an all-in-one solution that will help you always stay organized. It’s one of the most popular apps on the market, and students love it for a good reason: it streamlines scheduling and includes dozens of useful functions that will make the note-taking process easier than ever before.

 


The free version can search for text in images, allows you to clip websites, synchronizes all of your notes between different devices, and has a passcode lock. It also enables you to convert your handwritten notes into the regular text. The premium version which costs $69.99 per year also enables you to annotate your PDF files and forward your emails into the app. If the price of the pro version looks scary, here’s the good news: students get a 50% discount.

 

Although students are always overwhelmed with too many different tasks, there are many apps that can help you, making your everyday life in college or high school much easier. These apps will help you stay organized, plan your activities, take notes, and get enough sleep.
