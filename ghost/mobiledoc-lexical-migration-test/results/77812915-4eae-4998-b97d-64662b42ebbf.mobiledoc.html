Overview

1960 ushered in cleaner lines for the top-of-the-line Impala. Chevrolet reshaped and separated the "bat wing" tail fins and added 6 round taillights to the rear, a feature the car would become known for.

The 1960 Impala became the best-selling automobile in the US, and continued to hold that position for the next decade. It did this by producing 511,925 total.
