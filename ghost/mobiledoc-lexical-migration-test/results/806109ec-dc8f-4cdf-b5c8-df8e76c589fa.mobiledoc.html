The Nova remained much the same for 1969. Sales fell by half, and Chevy had dropped the Chevy II portion of the name leaving only 'Nova.'

Mid-year a low-cost option semi-automatic transmission based on the Powerglide called the Torque-Drive became available. It was only offered on the 4- and 6-cylinder engines. Another new option was the Turbo Hydra-Matic

The 1969 SS Novas were the first to have standard front disc brakes.

The most exciting news for Nova fans and performance seekers was the very limited Yenko Nova 427. These were Yenko dealer installed cars featuring a 427ci/450hp engine. They were built in both 1968 and 1969. Only 37 were built in 1969.
