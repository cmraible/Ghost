1963 marked the first year for the generation that would go on to be referred to as the mid-year Corvettes. Despite implications of mediocrity created by this name, the second generation Corvette was anything but. In fact, many enthusiasts believe that 1963 through 1967 model years are among the best for America’s Sports Car.

Thanks to Auburn Cord Duesenberg Automobile Museum in Auburn, Indiana, you can now enter to win a 1963 example packed with all of the features that made the mid-year Corvette so good. This matching numbers 1963 Riverside Red C2 Corvette features a 360-horsepower, fuel injected 327-cid small-block V8 paired to a 4-speed manual transmission and a fully independent suspension. Making this icon even more iconic, this example has a white soft top over a well equipped black leather interior.
