How Google uses sub-headings

This is a wierd one (as seems common with SEO blogs). The main article makes a bunch of assertions that aren't really backed up by the quotes from the Google employee he uses. In essence, the core message here is twofold:

  1. No, just putting keywords in headings won't help you rank better
  2. However, well-written sub-headings, which are informative to the reader, will also help Google understand the content better, and thus rank it for appropriate keywords.

In other words: use subheadings - but write them for the reader's benefit, and get the SEO benefit for free.
