The Print Tipping Point

Thomas Baekdal started an interesting thread on Mastodon about the decline of newspapers. He pointed out that, if you adjust for population growth, newspaper readership has been in steady decline since the 1960s, with a small acceleration thanks to the internet.

In other words, we can't blame the internet for what's happening to printed newspapers. And it might now be too late to save most of them, as Steve Yelvington pointed out in that thread:

But we are now at a point where the business model breaks. Printing is no longer economically sustainable, so you see newspapers cutting editions, outsourcing the distant plants, and eventually going online-only. And since the online business model will not deliver the cash necessary to sustain large newsrooms and the content they create, we are seeing precipitous localized dropoffs along with the long-term trends.
