Accelerated Mobile Pages (AMP)

Accelerated Mobile Pages are a direct competitor to Facebook’s instant articles, aiming to deliver a fast, mobile optimised experience especially when delivered via search. It’s an open source project, but is led by Google.

The project was launched in 2015, with AMP appearing in mobile search results from early 2016. The principle benefit for SEO purposes of publishing AMP versions of your pages is the chance to appear in the news carousel on news-related searches. This looks like this on desktop:

And like this on mobile:

The format is not universally popular. Some people claim remarkable traffic gains from it, others are less committed. However, it’s clear that if you want to appear in those newsy search results, Google will force you into deploying AMP.

AMP Stories is an emerging format, built on the AMP protocol. Only selected publishers have access for now.

The future of AMP

With Google removing the requirement for AMP to appear in the news carousel once the Core Web Vital metric comes into play, there is some suggestion that the AMP format may not be long for this world. It's a little early to say that for sure - but it's worth watching.
