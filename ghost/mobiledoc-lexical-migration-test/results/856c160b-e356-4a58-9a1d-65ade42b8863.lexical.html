TODAY’S STAT:

TODAY’S GOOD NEWS:

Xavier Becerra, Biden’s pick for HHS, is a Medicare for All supporter who's pushed for HHS to use rules on the books to seize patents in order to lower drug prices. He’s described himself as a “single-payer advocate all my life.” His selection comes after Wall Street darling Gina Raimondo removed her name from consideration.

TODAY’S FLASHBACK:

In 2012, Biden’s chief economic advisor Cecilia Rouse talked about how "we have to make choices about how we're going to spend our scarce dollars" and spoke positively about a balanced budget.

TODAY’S CLICKS:

  • YES, VACCINE MAKERS DO GET GOVERNMENT HELP: To justify charging Americans high prices for a COVID vaccine, drugmakers like Pfizer are holding themselves out as free-market success stories who need to recoup their R&D expenses. However, Big Pharma receives huge government subsidies and then returns the favor by charging Americans the world’s highest prices for medicine.
  • BLACKSTONE CEO GIVES ANOTHER $15 MILLION TO SENATE GOP: Billionaire Blackstone Group CEO Steve Schwarzman has now donated $35 million this election cycle to the Senate Leadership Fund, a super PAC working to keep the chamber red. Blackstone reported paying a negative tax rate in 2019, thanks to the GOP tax law.
  • SARA GIDEON’S DRY POWDER: Maine Senate candidate Sara Gideon lost a close race against vulnerable Republican Sen. Susan Collins. Her campaign still had $14.8 million in the bank after the November election.
  • BIDEN PROMISED TRANSPARENCY, BUT WILL HE DELIVER?: While federal law requires federal officials to disclose sources of income over $5,000, a loophole allows non-disclosure for information that is “confidential as a result of a privileged relationship, established by law.” A number of Biden’s nominees have come through WestExec Advisors, a consulting firm that does not disclose its clients.
  • CLIMATE CHANGE IS KILLING US ALARMINGLY QUICKLY: A new report published in The Lancet has found that extreme heat is killing Americans with no signs of stopping. The report found that in the last 20 years, that there has been a 50 percent increase in heat-related deaths among people over 65.
  • UN WARNS OF FAMINE IN YEMEN: A new report from the UN is urging action as the number of people facing catastrophic food insecurity in Yemen is expected to nearly triple by June, reaching as high as 47,000.
