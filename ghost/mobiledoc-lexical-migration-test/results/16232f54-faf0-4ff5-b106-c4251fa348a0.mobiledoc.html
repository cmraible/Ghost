For 1957, the Forward Look arrived, and suddenly it was 1960 with torsion-bar front suspension and TorqueFlite automatics. The '57 Plymouth was so new and striking that the advertisements proclaimed, "Suddenly it's 1960!"

The new model was 3-inches longer and 5.5 inches lower. It did retain the same white and gold side trim as the previous year but the anodized gold wheel covers were gone with gold grille bars being added.

Power now came from a larger 318ci V8. It produced 290hp with dual four-barrel carbs, special camshaft, high compression engine heads, heavy-duty valve springs and free-flowing dual exhausts.

The 3-speed manual was still standard but now buyers had the option of the now famous TorqueFlite.

A total of 7,438 Furys were produced when the car cost $2,925.
