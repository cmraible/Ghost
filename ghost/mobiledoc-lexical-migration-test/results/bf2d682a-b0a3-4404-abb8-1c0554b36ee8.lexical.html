This classic Mustang has been well cared for by its owner.

Broadway Motors in Kansas City, Missouri, receivers this 1966 Shelby Mustang GT350 new in June 1966. In November 1, 1966, it was purchased by its original owner. It’s reported that it stayed around the midwest over several owners, and received a restoration in 1983 before it was bought by its current owner who has owned the Shelby since 1996.

Finished in red with white stripes, the black interior of this Pony Car pull the whole ascetics together for a very cool and clean look. Powering the classic Stand is a 289 cubic-inch HiPo V8 with Holley 4-BBL carb and high-rise aluminum intake manifold. The 306-horsepower and 329 lbs-ft of torque are sent thought a Borg Warner T10 4-speed manual transmission to the rear wheels. The engine was rebuilt in 1983 and has been well-maintained since.
