The new Barracuda was completely restyled and no longer shared any kind of sheet metal with the Valiant, although under the skin the platform was still shared with that car. The engine bay was widened so the big-block 383 would fit.

Chrysler was clearly in a sales race with Ford (vs. the Mustang), so the new 1967 lineup included three body styles: the fastback, a notchback hardtop and a convertible.

The Formula S option package was still available and added a heavy duty suspension, tachometer, Wide-Oval tires, and special emblems and trim. As the pony car class became established and competition heated up, Plymouth division met the challenge head-on with a wide array of models and powertrain options.
