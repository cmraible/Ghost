Things That You Love To See:

• McDonald's Workers Will Strike for $15 an Hour in 15 Cities: “McDonald's cashiers and cooks in 15 U.S. cities will strike on May 19 — a day before the fast-food behemoth's annual shareholder meeting — to demand McDonald's pay all of its workers at least $15 an hour amid a national labor shortage in the fast food industry,” Vice reports.

• Jeff Bezos' Heirs Might Have To Pay The Taxes He Is Avoiding: If President Joe Biden’s tax plan becomes law as it is currently written, Jeff Bezos’ heirs would owe tens of billions of dollars in taxes on the money they inherit from him. That’s because Biden’s plan includes a repeal of the so-called step-up rule, which currently allows investors to pass on assets to their heirs without paying taxes on the capital gains from those assets.

• Congress Looks To Judicial Overrides To Strengthen Consumer Protections: Lawmakers are preparing to leverage the power of the judicial override to reverse a recent U.S. Supreme Court decision “that would limit the ability of the Federal Trade Commission to seek monetary relief for borrowers who have been defrauded by corporate lenders,” The Intercept reports. “With a simple tweak to the legislative text — which can pass on an individual basis or as part of an omnibus package — a so-called judicial override can address or eliminate whatever ambiguity the Supreme Court found within the law, thereby nullifying the court’s decision.”

• 68 Nobel Laureates Demand DOJ Review Prosecution of Steven Donziger: “Dozens of Nobel Laureates are demanding the U.S. Department of Justice immediately intervene and block the controversial misdemeanor contempt prosecution of U.S. human rights attorney Steven Donziger by a private Chevron law firm appointed by a judge with investments in Chevron,” according to a press release by Donziger’s defense team. Organizing Works:

• Bold Progressive State Tax Victories Provide Bright Spots in Difficult Year: States including New York, Washington, Maryland, New Mexico, and others have passed laws this year to make their tax codes more fair, including new taxes on the wealthy and on capital gains, as well as expanded tax credits for the middle class.

• Pros For The PRO Act: The sports unions of some the United States’ largest professional leagues have announced their support for the Protecting The Right To Organize Act, adding more momentum to the massive organizing push behind the landmark labor legislation,

• Nurses Are Striking Across the Country Over Patient Safety: Nurses in Worcester, Massachusetts, are currently on strike against the massive, for-profit Tenet Healthcare Corporation, in what is potentially the longest nurse’s strike in the past three decades. The strike is part of broader labor upheaval in the healthcare industry across the country right now, The Nation reports.

• Rhode Island Legislature Passes $15 Minimum Wage: The Rhode Island legislature has passed a bill raising the state’s minimum wage to $15 an hour. The legislation, if it is signed by the governor, would make Rhode Island the 9th state to put its minimum wage workers on the path to earning $15 an hour.
