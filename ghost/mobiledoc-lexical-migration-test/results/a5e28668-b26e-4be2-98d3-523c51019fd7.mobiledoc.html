Solar Out-Powers Oil

For the first time ever, solar energy investments have overtaken oil, according to the International Energy Agency.

In 2023, global investments in solar power projects will amount to $382 billion, while oil production will come in at $371 billion.

EPA To Close Loophole On Centuries Of Toxic Waste

The EPA is proposing tightened regulations for coal ash, the highly toxic waste that comes from burning coal.

Published as a draft rule last week, the proposal would close a huge loophole left by 2015 regulations from the Obama administration, which called for safe disposal of coal ash at new and existing facilities, but excluded all plants that were already closed. Since U.S. industries have been burning coal for two centuries, that loophole left out hundreds of facilities. Nearly half of all coal ash ever produced in the country wasn‚Äôt covered by the rule, including waste lingering in landfills, retired power plants, and other highly toxic sites, many of which are located in low-income communities of color.

Coal ash contains pollutants linked to cancer, heart disease, reproductive failure, and other severe health issues. What‚Äôs more, 91 percent of existing and retired coal plants that are currently being monitored are actively polluting groundwater at unsafe levels. Last year, journalists exposed the regulatory loophole and Earthjustice sued the EPA, leading to the settlement that brought this proposal.

If you want to weigh in and tell the EPA that you are also against hazardous toxins, they‚Äôre taking public comment on the proposal until July 17.
