Hey everyone…

Last week, our timing was almost hilarious — hours after I published a piece on why we should feel hopeful, Justice Ginsberg died, opening up the possibility of a radical conservative court for the next 50 years. It was a bit deflating, as I summarized here.

However, I just want to reiterate: I remain optimistic, even in these dark times.

I don’t think we are through the darkness. I think things are probably going to get more turbulent before they get better. But I also think we are experiencing an awakening like we haven’t seen in decades. The challenge is turning that awakening into constructive, progressive action.

Back in 2008, I wrote a book called The Uprising: An Unauthorized Tour of the Populist Revolt Scaring Wall Street and Washington. There was a central premise in the book: the public was becoming increasingly angry at a government and economy that was pulverizing the 99 percent, and politics would be defined by the left and right competing to channel that anger into political change.

I believe that hypothesis has been proven 100 percent correct — and I think unfortunately, we are seeing the right winning that competition right now. But that doesn’t mean the progressive movement is dead — far from it. I hope that what we are seeing on the right is a final so-called extinction burst, before we enter a new era of progressive change. And let’s remember: if there’s one thing this era has taught us, it is that change can happen very quickly.

Call me a hopeless optimist. Call me unrealistic. But that’s what I believe — and I believe it because that is the story of our country. Every major step of progress happened through tunnels of darkness. The process wasn’t pleasant or easy — but it happened because people kept fighting. That’s what this moment calls for — and that’s what we are focused on in this grassroots-funded media outlet that you are supporting.

Rock the boat,

Sirota
