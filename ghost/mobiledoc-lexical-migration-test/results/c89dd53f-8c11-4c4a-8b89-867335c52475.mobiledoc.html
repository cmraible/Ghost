The first-gen Camaro is an icon among classic car collectors in just about all forms, but cars equipped with the RS and SS packages always seem to be a litter more coveted. For a perfect example of this, just check out the 1968 Chevrolet Camaro RS/SS Convertible that is currently up for sale through Corvette Mike Midwest.

Looking as pristine as it did back in 1968, this Camaro RS/SS benefits from a full restoration finished in a striking Matador Red paint job with a black soft top. Thanks to the RS appearance package, the exterior is complemented by the hood stripe, hidden headlights and factory rallye wheels, while the SS package adds the fake chrome vents on the hood. Inside this Camaro, it's clear that no detail was overlooked in giving the cabin a period-correct look including the Houndstooth bucket seats and full-length center console, and one notable upgrade is the vintage-looking stereo system that is actually a modern AM/FM radio with hidden USB and Aux ports.

Under the hood, this 1968 Chevrolet Camaro is rocking the L34 396 cubic-inch V8 that is paired to a four-speed manual transmission. This engine packs a 350-horsepower punch, and thanks to an upgraded aftermarket exhaust system, you won't miss this big-block Camaro when it's approaching. Backing up its looks, this Camaro is sure to be an excellent cruiser thanks to power steering and power front disc brakes.
