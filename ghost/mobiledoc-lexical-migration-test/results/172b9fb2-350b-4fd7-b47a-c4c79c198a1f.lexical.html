Back in the 1950s, Cadillac was at the top of its game. The American luxury brand was considered to be one of the finest in the world. Anyone who was anyone drove a Caddie, and looking at this restored 1956 Cadillac Coupe DeVille, it’s not hard to see why.

After a body off frame thorough restoration, this Caddie is ready to show off. A white paint job and correct chrome details show off the curves and excellent fins just so. The red roof is also silky smooth, plus all the factory badges and trim pieces are in place, making for a smooth presentation. Wide whitewalls on the tires keep everything period correct.

A similar level of sumptuous details greet you inside. White and red two-tone seats match the exterior color scheme, only inverting them for a unique presentation. That pairing is featured on the door panels and dash as well, plus plenty of brightwork all around, including on the headliner, shows the level of luxurious detail Cadillac put into this car. All the factory controls and gauges are in place, making for an enviable level of preservation.

Powering this absolutely gorgeous machine is a 365ci V8. It puts out a nice 285-horsepower, which was considerable for its time, thanks to an increased stroke. This car also uses the Hydra-Matic 4-speed automatic transmission, which GM debuted in 1956. It boasts smooth gear transitions, representing GM’s engineering might for the time. This car is wonderful for driving slow through city streets, making it perfect to take out and participate in classic car cruise and parades. It’s also quite comfortable on the open road.

When this car launched for the 1956 model year, it carried a factory price of $4,624, which was a considerable amount back then. Cadillac made just over 24,000 of them, but few have survived. The unique and excellent opportunity to scoop this one, which is in fantastic condition, comes via Volo Auto Museum.
