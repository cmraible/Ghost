The TMI Weekend Reading List — 7/19/20

Stuff I’m reading:

• How to Let Things Slide (Forge) — The pandemic and social isolation is psychologically difficult for everyone. As a parent, this essay really spoke to me about coping.

• You’ll Probably Forget What It Was Like to Live Through a Pandemic (Vice) — Fascinating piece on the science of how memory works, and what will happen to our memories of this era over the long haul.

• America Should Prepare for a Double Pandemic (The Atlantic) — “Four months ago, it felt needlessly alarmist to fret about two rare events happening simultaneously. But since then, federal fecklessness and rushed reopenings have wasted the benefits of months of social distancing…My worry from March feels less far-fetched. If America could underperform so badly against one rapidly spreading virus, how would it fare against two?”

• Fracking Firms Fail, Rewarding Executives and Raising Climate Fears (NY Times) — “Oil and gas companies in the United States are hurtling toward bankruptcy at a pace not seen in years…And in the wake of this economic carnage is a potential environmental disaster — unprofitable wells that will be abandoned or left untended, even as they continue leaking planet-warming pollutants, and a costly bill for taxpayers to clean it all up. Still, as these businesses collapse, millions of dollars have flowed to executive compensation.”

• Inside John Roberts’ Decades-Long Crusade Against the Voting Rights Act (Politico) — To all those who spent the last few weeks praising John Roberts as a great heroic moderate defending the republic — please read this article. And make sure to notice the quote from Rep. John Lewis.

• A Powerful Law Gives HHS The Right To Take Control Of Remdesivir (Stat) — When it comes to the new treatment for Covid, it is important to remember that the government “holds important patent rights over remdesivir that it could use to take control of manufacturing and distribution, thereby accelerating competition and ensuring that everyone can access the drug.”

Stuff to watch/listen to:

• How Schumer, Dems Spent Millions To Crush Progressives In Primaries (Rising) — I talked to Krystal Ball and Saagar Enjeti about the big story TMI broke last week.

• Blowback (Stitcher) — I did a road trip to southern Colorado last weekend, and listened to some episodes of the new podcast called “Blowback.” It looks back on the Iraq War, providing some deep history and context — and also a bit of dark humor. You should definitely listen to this series.
