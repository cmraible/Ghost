THINGS THAT YOU LOVE TO SEE:

  • Senate Confirms Medicare For All Advocate For Top Health Job (Politico) — The Senate confirmed Xavier Becerra to serve as Health and Human Services Secretary this week. While in Congress, Becerra advocated for Medicare For All and pressed the Obama administration to force Big Pharma to lower drug prices, The Daily Poster previously reported.
  • Chevron Could Be Punished For Greenwashing (Earther) — “There may be trouble ahead for companies who claim to be helping the planet while continuing to produce fossil fuels. A coalition of green groups filed a complaint with the Federal Trade Commission on Monday alleging that Chevron is misleading the public in its claims that it is working to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.”
  • Democrats Are Preparing To Hike Corporate Tax Rate (Axios) — Democrats are preparing to undo one of the biggest legislative wins of the Trump administration: tax relief for corporations. As lawmakers begin to put together an infrastructure bill, President Joe Biden and other Democrats “are zeroing in on raising the corporate tax rate from its current 21% to 28%,” Axios reports.
  • Progressive Groups Ask Senators To Commit To No Corporate Lawyers For Biden’s Judge Picks(Huffington Post) — “If the American public is to maintain faith in our judicial system, it is critical that we restore balance to a bench where former corporate lawyers and prosecutors are so heavily overrepresented,” progressive organizations wrote in a letter to Democratic senators this week, demanding that they look beyond corporate lawyers when appointing judges.

ORGANIZING WORKS:

  • Medicare For All Bill Reintroduced On House Floor (Truthout) — “Two Democratic lawmakers, joined by over 100 cosponsors, introduced a Medicare for All bill on Wednesday, reinvigorating the call for single-payer health coverage in the United States they say is needed now more than ever.”
  • Union Drive at Alabama Amazon Warehouse Could Spur “Domino Effect” (The Seattle Times) — Labor leaders in Washington state, where Amazon is headquartered, believe that the union election currently underway at the Amazon warehouse in Bessemer, Ala., could encourage other Amazon warehouses to organize union drives, regardless of whether the Alabama workers vote to form the union. According to one local labor activist, the Alabama drive “will really help workers feel like there’s hope in organizing, hope in sticking together and building something better for the future.”
  • CDC Moves To Extend Eviction Moratorium (The American Prospect) — Following intense pressure from housing rights organizations, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention has proposed an extension of its eviction moratorium, which is set to expire on March 31. While the moratorium is imperfect and has let many renters slip through the cracks, “the extension of the order will prevent something much worse from happening,” The American Prospect reports.

The organization Red Canary Song, a grassroots collective of Asian and migrant sex workers has some great resources for those seeking more information, and is fundraising for the families of the victims online.
