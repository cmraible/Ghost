Overview

When all was said and done in 1974, the Barracuda had by far its worst sales year ever, and production was only 11,734, which also represented the lowest production number for any pony car.

When all was said and done in 1974, the Barracuda had by far its worst sales year ever, and production was only 11,734, which also represented the lowest production number for any pony car.

The only notable difference in the 1974 Barracudas and the 1973 models was that the 360 CID V8 engine option being available all year. It produced 245 horsepower, as opposed to the 240 produced by the previous 340 motor. As it had been the year before, the standard engine for Barracudas was a 318 CID V8 rated at 150 horsepower.

The 1974 Barracuda was discontinued 10 years to the day after the 1964 model had been introduced.
