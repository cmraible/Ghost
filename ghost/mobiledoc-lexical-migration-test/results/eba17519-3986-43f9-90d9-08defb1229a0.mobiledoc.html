Droptop Six Pack 1971 Plymouth 'Cuda Convertible

Throwback 2018 Ford GT 1967 Heritage Edition

It's good business to remind the world of the time you went up against Ferrari at Le Mans and beat them so badly at their own game that they went home with their rosso corsa painted tails tucked between their trembling legs. Both times that Ford has brought back the GT, they've leveraged that heritage in the form of special paint schemes.

This particular 2018 Ford GT is a '67 Heritage Edition. It's painted to resemble the 1967 car, which won Le Mans for the first and thus far only time that an all American team has won overall.

American Original 1953 Muntz Jet

Originally devised by famed Indy car builder Frank Kurtis, the Muntz Jet combined a sleek body, a powerful V8 (from Lincoln, in the case of this car), and an interior that was nothing short of opulent.

That interior featured a rear-mounted onboard ice chest and drinks compartment, while the front console is built around the car's radio. Apparently, these cars were so luxurious that Muntz lost $1,000 on each one built – a lot of money in the 1950s, especially considering that these cars cost just $4,500 new.

See it here.

We found this beautiful Silver Blue example right here on Motorious. It's being offered by Classic Cars of Sarasota for a very reasonable (by split window standards) $139,000.
