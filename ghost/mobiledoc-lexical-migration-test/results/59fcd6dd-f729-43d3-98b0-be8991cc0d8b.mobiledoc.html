Overview

The 1973 Corvette was the first to receive a significant restyle since the third-generation Corvettes were introduced in 1968, mainly due to the ever-increasing federal regulations.

The 1973 Corvette was the first to receive a significant restyle since the third-generation Corvettes were introduced in 1968, mainly due to the ever-increasing federal regulations. The front bumper was replaced with a federally mandated 5 mph bumper that made the car 35 lbs heavier. That means the Corvette had a rubber front bumper and chrome rear bumper.

Radial tires were introduced as well as a new coolant recycling system.
