Google has a number of approaches to indexing paywalled content.

Lead-in

With this option, the reader sees a snippet of the whole article, while the rest remains blocked to non-paying members. Google indexes the snippet alone and make ranking decisions based on that.

Metered

Google has a strong preference for metered paywalls, which allow it to index all content.

These both fall under Google's flexible sampling policy, which you can read here:

If you're unwilling or unable to go that route, the other option is to find a system that allows Googlebot (the search crawler) to see the content as it it was a member - but use markup to indicate to Google that it's paywalled content, so you don't fall foul of Google's ban on cloaking. There's a developer article explaining how to implement this in structured data.
