Good things are happening! The U.S. Postal Service and the City of Los Angeles are exploring setting up public financial institutions as an alternative to their private predatory counterparts. Financial officials around the world are closing in on a deal to set a global minimum tax rate, in order to stop the evasive tax practices of the world’s superrich. And the movement towards literary equity continues, as New York city libraries end all late fees.

All that and much more in this week’s You Love To See It, exclusively for paying subscribers below.

Things That You Love To See:

• Global Tax Deal Moves Closer as Hungary, a Low-Tax Holdout, Signs On (New York Times) — Following the leak of the Pandora Papers, a trove of documents that revealed the extent to which the world’s richest people are hiding their wealth in tax havens, the movement for a 15 percent global minimum tax rate is moving forward. “Finance ministers have been racing to finalize the agreement, which they hope will reverse a decades-long race to the bottom of corporate tax rates that have drained countries of the revenue that they need to build new infrastructure and combat global health crises,” the New York Times reports.

• A ‘Historic Event’: First Malaria Vaccine Approved by W.H.O.(New York Times) — “The world has gained a new weapon in the war on malaria, among the oldest known and deadliest of infectious diseases: the first vaccine shown to help prevent the disease. By one estimate, it will save tens of thousands of children each year.”

• ​​Pennsylvania Attorney General Josh Shapiro Charges Pipeline Company With 48 Environmental Crimes—“Attorney General Josh Shapiro, in conjunction with the 45th Statewide Investigating Grand Jury, today announced that the Office of Attorney General’s Environmental Crimes Section has charged Energy Transfer, L.P. (Energy Transfer), the corporate successor to Sunoco Pipeline L.P., with 48 counts of environmental crimes for their conduct during the construction of the Mariner East 2 Pipeline,” according to a press release from Shapiro’s office.

• ​​New York Public Library Ends All Late Fees(Washington Post) — “The New York Public Library system announced it would no longer charge late fees on overdue books and other resources and will waive existing fines in a move that will allow hundreds of thousands of people who had their library cards blocked to again check out reading materials… Before the announcement, about 400,000 New Yorkers’ library cards were blocked because they owed at least $15 in fines — and more than half of those people live in high-needs communities, officials said.”

Organizing Works:

• USPS Begins Postal Banking Pilot Program (The American Prospect) —
“The United States Postal Service (USPS) has taken the most dramatic step in a half-century to re-establish a postal banking system in America… The move puts the USPS in direct competition with the multibillion-dollar check-cashing industry, which operates storefronts to allow unbanked or underbanked residents to cash their paychecks.” We have the American Postal Workers Union to thank for the move: The postal banking pilots came out of a 2016 collective bargaining agreement that the postal workers union negotiated with the USPS.

• Los Angeles Takes Key Step Towards Establishing Public Bank(Los Angeles Times) — “After a long hiatus, the movement to establish a public bank in Los Angeles is once again inching forward. The City Council voted Tuesday to begin a process to study the viability of forming a city-owned bank and to create a business plan for doing so. Proponents say a public bank would allow the city to save money on banking fees, increase credit access for small businesses in underserved areas and help finance affordable housing and green energy programs.”

• How Rep. Pramila Jayapal Turned The Progressive Caucus Into A Powerful Force(Huffington Post) — “Normally, conservative Democrats, who are typically more willing than progressives to let legislation die, are the most adept at using hardball tactics to dictate the party’s agenda. But a unique confluence of events, plus years of organizing, prepared the [Congressional Progressive Caucus] to become an influential bloc on par with any other faction in the party.”

• Biden Restores Protections for Bears Ears National Monument(Earther) — “The Biden administration is using its executive powers to restore environmental protections to Bears Ears National Monument, an expansive, sacred landscape in Utah. The move comes four years after former President Donald Trump stripped protections away from Bears Ears, cutting back its area by 82%.”
