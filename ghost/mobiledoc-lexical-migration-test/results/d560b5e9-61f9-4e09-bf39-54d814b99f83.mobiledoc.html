Overview

The 1973 Mustang marked the end of the ‘big’ Mustang, as a smaller, lighter breed would replace it.

The 1973 Mustang marked the end of the ‘big’ Mustang, as a smaller, lighter breed would replace it.

Most changes were cosmetic like a large, square, chrome headlamp bezel, chrome trim around the taillights, a color-keyed urethane front bumper, and the front parking lights were now placed vertically in the standard front honeycomb grille. This would be the last convertible until 1983.

A new option for 1973 was the addition of the metallic Glow paint. The three colors were Gold Glow, Ivy Glow and Blue Glow. The 351 4V was the top of the line Power Plant for 1973.

The Mustang Grande was basically a luxury package and one could option in any engine. It came with such comfort features as vinyl Landau roof, power windows, wood-grain, and a rear window defroster.

The Mach 1 encased the 302ci V8 as standard. The 351 4V was the top of the line Power Plant for 1973. And a new honeycomb texture grille easily distinguished the Mach 1 from other ‘Stangs, as did the Mach 1 fender and rear decals.
