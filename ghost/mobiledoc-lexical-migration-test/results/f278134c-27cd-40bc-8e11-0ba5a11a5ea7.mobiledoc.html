In many ways the Mitsubishi 3000GT was a revolutionary model for the automaker. It was introduced in 1991 and featured a sporty look that really caught the attention of the younger crowd. Although the aerodynamic and sleek body lines with multiple vents and attractive spoiler options gave the initial impression that the car could be a heavy contender in the up and coming JDM segment, the car was actually pretty weighed down and, as a base model, a bit under powered.

To keep up with the competition, twin-turbo and all-wheel-drive variants were put forward and the results were attention-getting to say the least. It was these features that really spoke for the car and soon garnered an enthusiast following. In 1994 the car was redesigned, which resulted in better lighting, a sportier look, and more power. Unfortunately Mitsubishi pulled the 3000GT from its lineup in 1999 leaving those that missed the opportunity to get behind the wheel disappointed.
