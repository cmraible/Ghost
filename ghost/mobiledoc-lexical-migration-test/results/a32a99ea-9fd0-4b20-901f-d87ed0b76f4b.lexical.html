The Thunderbird’s success continued for quite some time. In 1959, the Thunderbird did not slow down in sales or popularity. It actually gained and by gained and the Thunderbird took flight. With sales numbers skyrocketing upward of 70,000 cars sold (67,456 to be exact) it was becoming an icon, somewhat of a status symbol for that generation. This car was not only selling like crazy, but it was making history by becoming Motor Trend’s first ever individual model to be given the title of “Car of the Year”.

Few things changed on the Thunderbird in 1959, a new grille was added and a new engine option for those who live by the motto “go big, or go home.” An optional Lincoln 430 V8, producing a pavement pounding 350 ponies (hp). The Thunderbird was Ford’s most expensive model offered for sale in ’59. Ford began offering leather seats in the Thunderbird to further personify the “Personal Luxury” feel that started the Thunderbird revolution.
