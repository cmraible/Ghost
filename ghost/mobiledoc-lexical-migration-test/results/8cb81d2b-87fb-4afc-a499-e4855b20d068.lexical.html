As the ‘72’s hit the streets, both emissions and safety were grabbing government (and insurance company) attention. In response, the big-block was gone from the ‘Cuda’s option sheet. It was a sad time in the muscle car era- no more underrating cars, fooling insurance companies; the man stepped in. It was time to get serious for the car companies and Plymouth was at the forefront of this with the Barracuda.

Big Block motors were discontinued in the 72 models of the Barracuda, still offered was the four speed with the pistol grip shifter. The only other major changes were there were a lot less options on the Barracuda, they removed the powered seats, power windows and interior upgrades to leather were dropped. One other point to be made was the quad headlights were removed and replaced with two large headlights.
