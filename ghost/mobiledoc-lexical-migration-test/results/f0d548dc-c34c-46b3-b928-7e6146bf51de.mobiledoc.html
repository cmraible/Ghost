Overview

The 1977 Corvette did receive minor changes, but they were more about branding than styling or performance.

The 1977 Corvette did receive minor changes, but they were more about branding than styling or performance. “Sting Ray” was dropped, and a new crossed flags badge adorned the front of the hood between the headlights as well as on the fuel filler door.

Some models were released with the original orange-colored engine, but GM was now pushing out blue-colored engines. Instruments were redesigned for better clarity, and a new leather wrapped steering wheel was offered. Power steering became standard. A cassette player was now also offered, and the manual shifter grew an inch, but shifts were shorter.
