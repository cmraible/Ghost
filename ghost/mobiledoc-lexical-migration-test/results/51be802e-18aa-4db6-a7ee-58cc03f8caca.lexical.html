The 1976 DeVille was the last year of its generation and offered new colors and options suggesting what was to come. A new finer crosshatched grille. Cornering lamps got new horizontal chrome trim while taillamps gained a new bold look. The Coupe DeVilles had a new vinyl roof whose top molding served as a continuation of the door "belt" molding.

A limited-slip differential was included for extra traction. Cadillac also offered Track Master, a computerized skid prevention system that automatically pumped the back brakes in an emergency situation to shorten the stopping distance.

The standard 500ci V8 (whose days were numbered) could have either a 4-bbl carb or fuel injection.

The d'Elegance returned and omitted the customary "V" and crest, substituting a distinctive stand-up see-through ornament along the chrome windsplit, among other luxurious amenities.

Cabriolet roofs had been available on Coupe DeVilles since 1974.

Production was up some 6,000 units.
