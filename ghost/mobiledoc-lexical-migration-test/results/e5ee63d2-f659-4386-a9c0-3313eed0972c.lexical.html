On The Daily Poster’s newest podcast, Sirota and Tom Frank discuss Frank's controversial Guardian op-ed about the power of elite groupthink and the pushback against scientifically reviewing the theory that the coronavirus leaked from an American-funded lab in China.

The discussion first begins with a review of some Daily Poster reporting this week on the housing market, and then moves into a deep-dive discussion of the lab-leak theory.

Though that theory has not been substantiated, new reporting has raised legitimate questions about so-called “gain of function” research related to viruses — and the Biden administration has now launched an inquiry. Frank’s column argues that if the lab-leak theory is proven true — or even partly true — it will be a huge blow against the entire notion of expertise, after a year in which political and media elites insisted that the hypothesis wasn’t even worthy of legitimate discussion.

To be sure, bad-faith right-wing actors have been trying to manipulate the story about the COVID-19 pandemic to gin up hostility against China — and if the Wuhan lab-leak theory has any merit, they will use that to try to fuel their dangerous political agenda. But here’s the key question: Should politically inconvenient facts and scientific inquiry be avoided just because bad-faith voices will inevitably try to distort them?
