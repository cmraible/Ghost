TODAY’S SCOOP:

The New York Times published a puff piece on Tuesday about former Sen. Heidi Heitkamp’s new role steering a dark money group seeking to protect a tax loophole she only months ago described as “one of the biggest scams in the history of forever” — and left literally all of that context out.

The Times described Heitkamp, a North Dakota Democrat, as “the smiling face of a well-financed effort to defeat a proposed tax increase that is crucial to funding the $3.5 trillion social spending bill at the heart of President Biden’s agenda.” The tax loophole Heitkamp is defending allows wealthy heirs to inherit investment assets without paying any capital gains taxes.

The paper wrote that Heitkamp “is adamant that taxation upon death, regardless of wealth, is deadly politics.” They further quoted her as saying her new job is “very consistent with my concern about revitalizing the Democratic Party in rural America.”

Unmentioned in the piece: Heitkamp is working for Saving America’s Family Enterprises (SAFE), a new dark money group incorporated in Delaware. It’s not clear at all who’s funding the group, or how “well-funded” they are. SAFE has released a few videos on YouTube, but good luck finding any television, radio, or digital ad buys. So far, their campaign has primarily centered around putting Heitkamp in front of friendly reporters and business TV talking heads.

The Times noted that Heitkamp has “counseled” her former colleagues to keep the tax loophole by using a threat, telling them to “understand there will be risk, and risk is the entire agenda.” She is not registered to lobby.

Worse still, the Times article neglected to mention that in April, Heitkamp specifically slammed the tax loophole she’s now being paid to defend. "This is one of the biggest scams in the history of forever on income redistribution," Heitkamp said, responding to former New Jersey Gov. Christie (R). "If you have a stock, you can pass it on to your kids with stepped-up basis, and it's never taxed. You know that there needs to be reform on unearned income. And so to demonize it and say it's going to hurt the little guy, yes, that just is not factual, Chris. And you know it."

In 2019, Heitkamp helped launch a rural-focused advocacy, the One Country Project, with the help of the bipartisan lobbying firm Forbes Tate Partners, where her former chief of staff is now a partner. Lobbying records show Forbes Tate recently registered to lobby for SAFE.

Heitkamp told the Times she was recruited to the effort by former Rep. John Breaux, D-La., who’s a director at the lobbying firm Crossroads Strategies. Lobbying records list SAFE’s address as the same office on Crossroads Strategies’ website.

The study found that warming oceans have depleted the whales’ primary food source, fatty crustaceans. As a result, the whales have been moving into waters where they collide with human vessels and fishing gear, which have been deadly. In the past decade, the whale population has declined by 26 percent.
