Facebook is about to decide if you're a journalist or not

Long-term readers will be familiar with my amusement watching social platforms tie themselves in knots trying to decide who is a journalist — and who isn't.

Well, it looks like Facebook is about to do that again.

It appears that it's going to be an inherited system: if you managed to get your Facebook Page through the verification process to be registered as a News Page, people working for that organisation can get journalist verification.

I'm not quite sure where that will leave freelancers, though. Given that they are the most vulnerable group of journalists, lacking the weight of a decent-sized publisher to back them up, that does seem like a serious oversight in a scheme whose stated purpose is to offer journalists more protection…

Indie Media: Ernest Journal crowdfunding

Ernest Journal isn't new - but it's indie print media at its best. It's a great magazine for the the outdoors enthusiast, who is more curious than adrenaline-fueled — which describes me perfectly. Which is why I have a complete set:

The editor had built an interesting new model of partnering with one major sponsor each issue to help fund each issue — and then saw all that vanish when the COVID crisis hit. She's now running a crowdfunder to fund the production of issue 10. They funded in under 24 hours(!), and are now working on stretch goals to support the magazine into the future.

Well worth chucking some cash their way, if you love print and the great outdoors.
