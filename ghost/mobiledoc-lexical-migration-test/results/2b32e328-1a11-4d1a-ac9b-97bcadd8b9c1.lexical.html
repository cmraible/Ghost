GOOD NEWS

• If We Get Carbon Emissions To Zero, The Climate Will Stabilize(Inside Climate News) –– “The idea that global warming could stop relatively quickly after emissions go to zero was described as a ‘game-changing new scientific understanding’ by Covering Climate Now, a collaboration of news organizations covering climate.”

• Maybe Public Pensions Will Finally Divest From Blackstone(The Daily Poster) –– Blackstone CEO Steve Schwarzman bankrolled Trump and many of the GOP politicians who attempted to overturn the election this week. The good news is that public officials can hold Schwarzman accountable by divesting public pension funds from Blackstone’s management, a move that politicians like Pennsylvania Treasurer Joe Torsella have signalled they might be willing to consider.

• House Democrats Are Moving To Impeach Trump(Washington Post) –– House Democrats are introducing articles of impeachment against Trump on Monday, which is a crucial first step towards holding the president accountable for inciting violence and attempting to overturn the election this week.

• Momentum for $2,000 Checks Is Building(Washington Post) –– President-elect Joe Biden is putting together a relief package that includes $2,000 stimulus checks, and even the centrists are on board.

ORGANIZING WORKS

• The Sunrise Movement Is Wielding Power In The Biden Administration(CNN) — Youth climate movement activists have won key victories when it comes to Biden Administration appointments, including Deb Haaland and Gina McCarthy for Interior and the Environmental Protection Agency, respectively.

• How Georgia Got Organized(The American Prospect) — The story behind Democrats picking up two Senate seats in Georgia this week is about Stacey Abrams, but it’s also about local grassroots organizations, largely led by Black women, who have been organizing for years to fight voter suppression and turn out new voters in the state.

• Unions Played A Key Role In Democrat Victories In Georgia(The Progressive) — “In the Georgia Senate runoff elections on January 5, Democrats Jon Ossoff and the Reverend Raphael Warnock unseated Trump-supporting incumbents Kelly Loeffler and David Perdue by margins of less than 80,000 votes—abetted by what a UNITE HERE! leader calls an ‘unprecedented ground game’ by labor unions.”

TAKE ACTION

• Tell Congress not to wait on impeachment (Demand Progress).

• Tell the Biden administration to commit to investigating Trump (Free Speech For People).

• Tell the Biden administration to protect immigrant workers who report labor violations (National Day Labor Organizing Network).
