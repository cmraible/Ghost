Good things are happening! The Debt Collective is organizing to cancel all kinds of debt and just notched a huge victory that could change the lives of thousands of people on probation. Georgians elected two Democratic senators on the promise of expanded health insurance coverage, and they could get just that if Biden’s compromise reconciliation bill passes. And the city of Chicago is using American Rescue Plan aid to send cash to thousands of households each month.

All that and much more in this week’s You Love To See It, exclusively for paying subscribers below.

Things That You Love To See:

• Guaranteed Basic Income Is Coming To Chicago (Chicago Tribune) — The Chicago City Council voted this week to use federal funds from the American Rescue Plan to set up a guaranteed basic income pilot program. Five thousand households will receive $500 per month for a year, making it one of the largest such programs in the country.

Tip Jar

• Bill To End Debt-Based Driver’s License Suspensions Moves To Senate Floor — A bill to “relieve hundreds of thousands of people from the predatory practice of debt-based driver’s license suspensions” passed out of committee this week and now is headed to the full Senate for a vote.

• Lina Khan Isn’t Worried About Going Too Far (New York Magazine) — Lina Khan, the new chair of the Federal Trade Commission, “is now indisputably the most powerful figure in the anti-monopoly vanguard,” New York Magazine reports. “Those around Khan say she’s more important than any one case. To them, she’s a vehicle for the courage to stand up to the corporations that have been bullying Americans ever since regulators abdicated their power, and they hope others follow her lead.”

Organizing Works:

• How To Cancel $3.2 Million Of Debt(The Intercept) — “The Rolling Jubilee Fund launched in 2012 as an offshoot of the Occupy Wall Street movement, initially focused on canceling medical, tuition, and credit card debt. The effort went dormant over the last five years as activists turned their sights to other projects they felt would bring about more systemic change. On Friday, the group announced a return to extinguishing debts outright, saying it had canceled $3.2 million in probation debt as ‘an act of solidarity’ amid the COVID-19 pandemic. In the portfolio purchased by the group, the average debtor owed $159. The Rolling Jubilee Fund had been able to purchase all of it for just $97,922.”

• Georgia Senators Deliver On Campaign Promise With Medicaid Expansion (Capitol Beat) — “Both U.S. Sens. Jon Ossoff and Raphael Warnock have been vocal proponents of Medicaid expansion in Georgia. Biden’s Build Back Better proposal includes a provision to close the Medicaid coverage gap in Georgia and the 11 other states where states have refused to expand Medicaid. Warnock’s office said the expansion will provide health insurance to about 646,000 Georgians who make too much money to qualify for traditional Medicaid coverage but make too little money to afford coverage on the marketplace.”
