Stuff I’m reading:

• Private Hospitals, Now Demanding Bailouts, Lobbied to Defeat Cost-Saving Health Reform As Coronavirus Crisis Grew (The Intercept) — The health care industry is spending big to try to defeat even modest measures to lower the cost of medical care during the pandemic.

• Coronavirus Reminds Us What Functioning Communities Look Like (Quartz) — The pandemic is awful, but it is prompting a new sense of community and solidarity, and that’s a good thing.

• Teaching Billionaire Bullies A Lesson (SEIU Local 105) — A labor leader outlines how the pandemic is laying bare the true motives and pathologies of the billionaire class.

• Bernie Lost Because America Doesn’t Have a Strong Labor Movement (In These Times) — Unions have played a pivotal role in almost every step of progress in American history. The relative weakness of today’s labor movement is a huge problem that must be solved. We need stronger unions.

• The Secret Battle to Strip Your Coronavirus Rights (TYT) — “While Bank of America and other companies present a supportive face, some are working behind your back to make it harder to seek justice for corporate wrongdoing related to the coronavirus.”

• ‘Murder Hornets’ in the U.S.: The Rush to Stop the Asian Giant Hornet (NY Times) — This bug sounds like something out of a horror film, only it is real, and it may have arrived here in the United States.

• With Live Sports Gone, Announcer Offers Play by Play of the Everyday (NY Times) — Many people are improvising and innovating during the quarantine — and that includes British sports announcers.

Stuff to Watch & Listen To:

• Amazon and employee safety (The Hill) — I was on The Hill’s morning show Rising to talk about our big scoop this week about how Amazon killed a shareholder resolution designed to protect workers’ safety.

• Everything Is Alive (Radiotopia) — I find this podcast to be funny, warm and soothing, which is exactly the content I need every now and again during the quarantine.
