Facebook's online mall announced

Mr Zuckerberg's big news for last night is, indeed, big. However is a very mixed piece of news. It would be churlish to deny that this is a short-term good, but it is also quite likely a long-term harm.

  • It's great news for small retail businesses, especially those that are in enforced shutdown and having to pivot to delivery. They will be able to get up and trading much more quickly.
  • It's bad news for the rest of the internet ecosystem, as Facebook strengthens its chokehold, and makes Facebook a substitute for the open internet for more and more people. We should all be very afraid of that happening.

Here's a suggestion: take advantage of it for now, if it's helpful to helping your business — or your client's — survive the crisis. But plan for an reduction strategy, where you move those customers into a more direct relationship.

Facebook has proved repeatedly that it'll eventually charge you more to reach the customers you've acquired.
