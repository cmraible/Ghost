France Sheds A Barrier To Climate Action

France is now the latest country to withdraw from the Energy Charter Treaty (ECT), a Cold War-era agreement that international fossil fuel interests have used to argue that climate change policies are cutting into their profits and demand that governments compensate them for their loss.

Italy was the first country to withdraw from the ECT in 2016, with Spain, Poland, the Netherlands, and now France following suit. The growing number of countries withdrawing from the treaty will help strip fossil fuel companies of their power to block or discourage drilling bans and climate planning.
