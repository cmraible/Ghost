Chrysler spent big money in the 1950s--some $300 million--restyling its 1957 models. The 1958 Fury thus looked much the same as its predecessor. It retained the Fury's distinctive color scheme and gold anodized grille, but got four headlights, "lollipop" taillights and wheel covers with gold centers. It was priced at $3,067.

The 290-horsepower V-8 was standard, but you could order a "Golden Commando" 350-cubic-inch V-8 that broke the 300-horsepower barrier with 305 horsepower. With optional, then-exotic fuel injection, that engine kicked out 315 horsepower.

A bad recession that affected all automakers held down production of the 1958 Fury to 5,308 cars. But the 1956-58 Fury was such a success in creating a sporty image for Plymouth that it put the Fury name on its top-line standard-series 1959 coupe and sedan models.

Decades later, author Stephen King selected the 1958 Fury to star in "Christine," and that says a lot about what a sexy car it was--and still is.
