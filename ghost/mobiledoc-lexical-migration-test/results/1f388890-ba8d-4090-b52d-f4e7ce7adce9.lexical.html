When it came to the Ford Mustang, the 1979 model year kicked off the Fox-body era with a solid run of 14 years as the 1993 model capped off the pony car's third-generation. As the Fox Mustang falls deeper into classic car territory, these cars are becoming more of a hot commodity. Street Dreams is offering the opportunity to own this limited-edition 1993 Ford Mustang LX Convertible feature car, also known as the Summer Edition.

Only 3,003 of these Fox-body feature cars were produced by Ford Motor Company in just two color options - Vibrant White or Canary Yellow. While 1,503 feature cars were sprayed in the bold monochromatic yellow, this is just one of 452 out of those equipped with a 5-speed manual transmission and black interior.

Exterior-wise, this adult-owned gem features a unique rear spoiler and a black power convertible top. To make the bold color pop even more, this retro Mustang is outfitted with a set of feature car-specific chrome wheels on all four corners.

Stuffed inside the engine bay of this Mustang is a 302-cubic-inch 5.0-liter HO V8 engine capable of a healthy 225-horsepower. A 5-speed manual transmission twists the Traction-Lok axle which spins the rear wheels. Adding to the performance and eight-cylinder symphony is a sport-tuned dual exhaust. Power front disc brakes help bring everything to a halt, and power steering makes taking those corners a breeze. Handling on this Mustang is kept in check thanks to a quad-shock rear suspension. In addition, this car comes with the convenience of power windows and power locks.

An immaculate black leather interior is the perfect contrast to this pony's bright yellow exterior. Interior highlights include the 'pony' headrests, special floor mats, a leather-wrapped steering wheel, power driver's seat with power lumbar support, cruise control, and electric remote mirrors. While the car does retain the factory AM/FM cassette with Premium Sound, it was upgraded with a trunk-mounted Alpine 6-disc CD changer complete with remote. Even better, when the top is up on this 'Stang, the creature comfort of air conditioning keeps everything inside nice and cool.
