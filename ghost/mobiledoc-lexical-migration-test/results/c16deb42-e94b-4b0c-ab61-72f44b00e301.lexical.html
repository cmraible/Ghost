The 1962 DeVille was basically a toned-down '61 with GM settling into a styling groove. Rear fins were now lower and less extravagant, a tamer grilled, and backup/turn/stop lights combined behind a single white lens for a cleaner look.

Four-window sedans received more orthodox rooflines but still included a pair of short-deck variants, which were now named Series 62 Town Sedan and DeVille Park Avenue.

A new braking system was introduced with dual master cylinder and separate front and rear hydraulic lines.

Apparently, the public liked the new, tamer styling and sales were up some 23,000 over '61.
