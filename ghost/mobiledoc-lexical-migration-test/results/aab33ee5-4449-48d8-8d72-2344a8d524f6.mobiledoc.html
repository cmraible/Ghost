After decades trapped in a New York parking garage, the legendary Lost Corvettes have resurfaced. Now, it's your chance to own one of these rare machines, and it'll only cost you the price of a raffle ticket.

This beautiful 1956 Corvette is one of just 290 cars painted in Cascade Green. That's fewer than ten percent of the total production run of 3,467.

1956 marked the first year that Corvettes featured a V8 as the base engine, replacing the original "Blue Flame" six cylinder. Two V8s were offered, including a 240 horsepower variant with dual carburetors.

This Corvette is powered by the base 210 horsepower 265 cubic inch small block V8, fed by a single four barrel carburetor. Transmission options in 1956 were a close ratio three speed manual and a two speed Powerglide automatic.
