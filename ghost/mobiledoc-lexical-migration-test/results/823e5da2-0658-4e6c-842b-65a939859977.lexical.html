Overview

The 1975 Monte Carlo was revised only slightly from the previous year like a new grille with the Monte Carlo emblem moved to the center section and new vertically shaped taillights with horizontal louvers.

The 1975 Monte Carlo was revised only slightly from the previous year like a new grille with the Monte Carlo emblem moved to the center section and new vertically shaped taillights with horizontal louvers.

Catalytic converters were introduced as standard equipment to meet federal emissions requirements. It came with advantages like improved fuel economy and drivability, but required more expensive and lower-octane unleaded gasoline. The disadvantage was a loss in power, however.

Also new for 1975 was a Custom interior option that included a plusher cloth 50/50 bench seat with recliner on passenger side and lower door panel carpeting. A new gauge as part of the "Econominder" became optional that showed if one was using too much gas became optional.

Sales dropped due to an increased price and stiff competition from the Charger SE, but production would bounce back in 1976.
