The Ford Model T is a vehicle that carries a lot of historical significance. Made from 1908 through 1927, a lot happened in the world around the Model T, and thanks to the Model T giving Americans the freedom to travel around. Regarded as the first affordable automobile, the Model T represents the advancements made at Ford for assembly line production and more efficient fabrication. They’ve now become a favorite for hot rodders, as evident by this Hemi powered 1927 Ford Model T.

This Metallic Black 1927 Ford Model T will turn heads, that’s if you can catch it before it zooms off. It’s powered by a monstrous 392 cubic inch Chrysler Hemi with dual quad Edelbrock 500 cfm carburetors and an Offenhauser Hi-rise intake. The engine has a solid lifter cam and exhales through a big exhaust.

The work on this unique machine was combing by Machinist and owner Dave Hughes. It has only 200 miles on the clock, and you can take it home thanks to Lucky Collector Car Auctions.
